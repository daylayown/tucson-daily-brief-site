# Section Naming — Brainstorm

Working doc. Not a decision yet. Picking back up 2026-05-22+.

## The problem

The site's section names are split between two registers, and the mix reads as inconsistent:

| Brand-flavored (good) | Generic verbs / file-cabinet (bad) |
|---|---|
| The Old Pueblo Speaks | Spotted |
| The Tucson Mini | Meeting Watch |
| | News Reports |
| | Ask |

The brand names make the site feel like it has a voice and a place. The verbs make it feel like a CMS. The new running-file section (the "tracking" idea from 2026-05-21 — see CLAUDE.md → Roadmap → Tracking) is the natural moment to set a Tucson-flavored pattern, and to consider whether Spotted gets renamed to match.

User's framing (2026-05-21): "we're just throwing out random verbs haha. maybe they can have some tucson flavor to them?"

## Avoid collisions with existing Tucson outlets

Real outlets in the Tucson market — do not pick a name that overlaps:

- **Tucson Sentinel** (tucsonsentinel.com) — nonprofit news, founded 2009. Killed "The Sentinel" as a candidate.
- **Tucson Spotlight** (tucsonspotlight.org) — nonprofit news. Kills "Spotlight."
- **Arizona Daily Star** — daily paper. Kills "Star," "Daily."
- **Tucson Weekly** — alt-weekly. Kills "Weekly."
- **Inside Tucson Business** — kills "Inside."
- **Tucson Local Media / Explorer News** — kills "Local," "Explorer."
- **AZPM (Arizona Public Media)** — public radio/TV.

Check any candidate against this list before committing.

## Candidates for the running-file / standing-topic section (currently "Tracking")

### Leading: Mirador (or "The Mirador")

- Spanish for "lookout / viewpoint." Literally a place from which you watch developments.
- Real architectural feature in Sonoran/Mexican adobe construction — upper-level watchtower-rooms built for surveying the landscape. Grounded in regional building tradition, not invented.
- Pairs cleanly with the masthead "From the Old Pueblo" — the mirador is part of the old pueblo, the watchtower of the historic Presidio.
- Single distinctive word, becomes a brand identifier.
- URL: `/mirador/`. On-page H1: "Mirador: Karla Toledo's ICE Detention." Reads beautifully.
- Doesn't collide with any Tucson outlet I'm aware of.
- **Risk:** a slice of readers won't recognize the word on first read. Mitigated by the fact that the site already leans Spanish-bilingual in voice (Old Pueblo, La Cañada, Cañada del Oro, etc.), and the section's content quickly teaches readers what it is.

### Fallbacks

- **The Almanac** — running-record, warm-literary. Astronomers/farmers keep almanacs. Fits the warm-organic editorial voice. Less specifically Tucson.
- **The Presidio** — the 1775 Spanish fortress that founded Tucson. Historical weight, archival flavor. Reads more "vault / institutional record" than "watching things unfold." May be better if the section's vibe is more "definitive reference" than "live updates."
- **The Catalinas** — the iconic mountain range to the north. Watching from elevation. Probably too literal/geographic.
- **Sky Island** — Tucson's term for isolated mountain ecosystems. Each tracked story IS a self-contained ecosystem of info. Interesting metaphor but obscure to non-locals.
- **El Vigía** — "the watcher / sentry" in Spanish. Evocative, but it's a person rather than a place, and more obscure than Mirador.

### Rejected during this brainstorm

- **The Sentinel** — Tucson Sentinel exists.
- **Tracking** — original placeholder, just a verb, no Tucson flavor.

## Spotted — possible rename

Harder than the running-file section because Spotted's function is so specific ("found this buried in a 200-page agenda; surfaced it"). Any metaphor has to do real work.

### Candidates

- **The Wash** — monsoon arroyos surface what was buried; after a storm, the water runs through a dry riverbed and you suddenly see everything in it. Maps cleanly to the function. **Risk:** "wash" has other meanings (a beer, a betting term), and "The Wash" may be opaque to non-locals on first read. "Spotted" at least telegraphs its function.
- **El Hallazgo** — "the finding / the discovery" in Spanish. Echoes Mirador's voice if we go Spanish across the brand. Genuinely captures Spotted's function (something discovered, surfaced from where it was hidden). Risk: stronger reader-comprehension hit than Mirador.
- **Caliche** — the hardpan layer just beneath the desert topsoil; what's buried but close to the surface. Obscure word; only locals know it.
- **The Margins** — generic newspapery, not Tucson.

### Tradeoff

Renaming Spotted now means cycling URLs, internal naming, and reader recognition for a section that's only ~6 weeks old and just stabilized. Could also wait — set the pattern with Mirador, and rename Spotted only if the Spanish/Tucson direction proves out and a clear winner emerges.

## News Reports — possible rename (further future)

If the brand direction lands, this might eventually become **Dispatches** or **Field Reports**. Defer until Spotted's rename is settled.
