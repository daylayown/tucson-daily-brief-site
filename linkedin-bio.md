## About section

I'm a 20-year tech journalist who builds the AI tools journalists use.

At Consumer Reports, where I'm a Senior Reporter, I ship production AI systems for our editorial team alongside my regular reporting on hardware, WiFi, and AI policy. So far that's included a TTS audio pipeline that cut production time by ~80%, a daily competitive intelligence brief for the Home Team, a weekly content freshness monitor, and a custom Manifest V3 browser extension that solved our editorial price-tracking problem after conventional scraping kept failing. All built solo with Claude Code, all in active editorial use, all instrumented for cost.

Outside CR I run **Tucson Daily Brief** (tucsondailybrief.com) — an AI-assisted civic journalism platform covering the Tucson metro. It's a daily news brief, plus AI-assisted agenda mining across four municipal governments (each on a different civic-tech vendor stack), plus a live AI meeting reporter built on Deepgram + Claude that captures local government livestreams in real time, plus a human-reviewed news report pipeline. The whole operation runs in production for under $10/month. It's a working answer to the question *"what does AI in local news look like when it's done with real editorial guardrails?"*

I build other things for fun, too: **Deep Dugout** (deepdugout.com), an AI-managed baseball simulation that runs all 30 MLB teams with Claude-powered managers; **Crosswording the Situation**, a daily news-themed mini crossword; a cross-platform mobile dream journal app; and several other things you'll find at daylayown.org.

Where my head is at right now: I think the most consequential AI work in newsrooms over the next decade is going to come from working journalists who learn to ship — not from tech executives parachuted in from outside the industry. The reverse direction is much harder than people assume, and the failure modes are editorial, not technical. I'm interested in being part of making the journalist-builder path the obvious one rather than the rare one.

I'm open to conversations about AI integration, editorial AI, and newsroom innovation roles at media companies, foundations, and academic institutions — US or international, remote-friendly. Bilingual Spanish. Based in southern Arizona, relocatable for the right opportunity.

Career stops along the way: TechCrunch, News Corp's The Daily, Circa News, Vice/Motherboard, and Consumer Reports. NYU '08.
