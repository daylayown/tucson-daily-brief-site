🚨 Public Safety

Community rallies after 3-year-old killed in street racing crash. Thousands of Tucsonans attended a tearful vigil Sunday for a 3-year-old girl named Anna, killed in a street racing incident, calling on city leaders to crack down on the dangerous activity. Her death has become a rallying point for residents demanding stronger enforcement.

Arizona Daily Star

Westside residents push for action as gunfire from state trust land endangers homes. Residents along Tucson's west side say illegal shooters on closed Arizona State Land Department trust land are firing weapons whose shells are striking homes, with one neighbor warning "Cinco de Mayo is going to be like a war zone." The ASLD closed the land to recreation in 2019, but chains and no-trespassing signs have not stopped the activity; a Pima County community meeting is scheduled for April 30 to discuss enforcement and physical barriers.

Democratic challenger far outraises Rep. Ciscomani in CD-6 race. A Democratic challenger to Republican incumbent Rep. Juan Ciscomani has significantly outraised him in early 2026, with both campaigns funded primarily by large donors, according to new FEC filings. Ciscomani, who represents the Tucson-area 6th Congressional District, received Republican House leadership cash infusions this cycle but still faces a notable fundraising gap heading into the November midterms.

🏗️ Development & Business

Historic Louis' Market to become Center for Cultural Organizing in Southside Tucson. The historic Louis' Market in downtown Tucson is being transformed into a resident-led Center for Cultural Organizing, run by the Southwest Folklife Alliance in partnership with Regeneración. The space is designed for community gathering, cultural celebration, and exchange.
