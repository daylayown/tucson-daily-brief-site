July 20, 2026

Monday

🚨 Public Safety

Downtown Tucson mass shooting injures nine; TPD officer shoots fleeing suspect. A mass shooting in downtown Tucson early Sunday morning left nine people injured, and a Tucson Police Department officer shot a suspect who was running from the scene; the suspect was transported to a hospital in critical condition. Witnesses described spotting the gunman outside a pizza restaurant before shots were fired, and one family reported narrowly escaping the area beforehand. Arizona leaders issued public statements in response to the shooting.

KVOA, KOLD, KGUN 9

TPD responds to barricade situation near Prince and Treat. Tucson Police, along with SWAT and the Hostage Negotiations Unit, responded Sunday evening to a barricade situation near the intersection of Prince Road and Treat Avenue, according to KOLD. No further details on the outcome were immediately available.

KOLD

Two men injured in shooting at McCormick Park. Tucson Police are investigating a shooting that occurred early Sunday morning near McCormick Park in midtown Tucson. According to authorities, one man was shot and a second sustained blunt force trauma; both were transported to a hospital.

KGUN 9, KVOA, KOLD

Three bodies recovered from waterways near Nogales and Tucson following monsoon flooding. Two bodies were pulled from the Nogales Wash north of Rio Rico on Saturday, bringing the total recovered from that wash to three over two days of monsoon flooding. Separately, the body of a missing Tucson man was found in the Santa Cruz River just south of Tucson.

Arizona Daily Star, Green Valley News

🎓 Education

Vail Unified School District students return to class Monday. Students in the Vail Unified School District headed back to school today, July 20, marking the first day of the new academic year for the district, according to KVOA.
