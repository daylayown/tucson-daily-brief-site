🚨 Public Safety

Inmate dies at Cochise County jail hours after court appearance. The Cochise County Sheriff's Office is investigating the death of a 45-year-old man who died while in custody at the Cochise County Jail. The man died just hours after appearing in court; circumstances of his death have not yet been released publicly.

🏛️ Government

Report: Pima County Sheriff Nanos improperly used position and resources against political rival. An administrative review by a Tucson firm concludes that Sheriff Chris Nanos improperly used his position and county department resources for "political gain" when he suspended a jail commander and political rival three weeks before the 2024 primary election. The summary of the review was obtained by the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

Federal government sues Arizona over prediction market regulation. Three weeks after Arizona Attorney General Kris Mayes indicted online prediction market company Kalshi on 20 criminal counts for allegedly violating state gambling laws, the Trump administration's Commodity Futures Trading Commission sued Arizona, claiming exclusive federal jurisdiction over prediction markets. The CFTC simultaneously filed suits against Connecticut and Illinois; if successful, the federal action could invalidate state gambling laws as applied to federally regulated prediction markets.

Arizona Mirror, Arizona Daily Star

Tucson invested more than $160 million in climate action last year. City leaders held a public roundtable Thursday on Tucson's climate action plan, disclosing that the city spent over $160 million on related initiatives in 2025. The session highlighted ongoing commitments to energy efficiency and infrastructure resilience as Southern Arizona faces continued heat pressures.

KVOA News 4 Tucson

Candidate fields finalized for Oro Valley and Marana municipal elections. Fields are now set for mayoral and town council races in both Oro Valley and Marana following the close of candidate filing. Voters in both communities will decide on local representation later this year.

🏗️ Development & Business

Renovations underway at historic Tucson House on Oracle Road. Renovations at the Tucson House, 1501 N. Oracle Road, began March 10 with a celebration attended by Gov. Katie Hobbs and Tucson Mayor Regina Romero. The aging residential high-rise — a long-standing Tucson landmark — is being redeveloped to bring new life to the North Oracle corridor.

Inside Tucson Business

Oro Valley weighing $1.86/month base water rate increase. The Town of Oro Valley is considering raising its monthly base water rate by $1.86, potentially effective in early July. The proposal comes as the town also reports a decline in the number of high-volume residential water users, affecting utility revenue projections.

Tucson Local Media

Legacy Learning to open Tucson campus for Arizona Schools for the Deaf and Blind students. Private education institution Legacy Learning Community is set to open a new Tucson campus in Fall 2026 that will welcome students with visual and auditory impairments, following the announced closure and relocation of the Arizona Schools for the Deaf and Blind. The campus will also serve general special education students.

🎉 Community & Events

Logan Phillips named Tucson Poet Laureate for 2026–2029. The City of Tucson, in partnership with the Arts Foundation for Tucson and Southern Arizona and the University of Arizona Poetry Center, has appointed Logan Phillips as the city's poet laureate for a three-year term. The position is supported by a coalition of civic and academic arts organizations.

Inside Tucson Business

UA Wildcats advance to Final Four, set to face Michigan in Indianapolis. The University of Arizona men's basketball team is in the NCAA Tournament Final Four for the first time in years, preparing to play the Michigan Wolverines in Indianapolis. The run has ignited enthusiasm in Tucson, with local businesses gearing up for watch parties and the city celebrating the Wildcats' deep playoff run.

Arizona Daily Star, KVOA News 4 Tucson

Sunnyside grad Sergio Vega wins undefeated NCAA Division I wrestling championship. Tucson's Sunnyside Unified School District celebrated alumnus Sergio Vega, who captured the NCAA Division I wrestling national title competing for Oklahoma State University while going undefeated on the season. Sunnyside held a special ceremony honoring Vega's historic achievement this week.

KVOA News 4 Tucson

Pima Air and Space Museum celebrates 50th anniversary. The Pima Air and Space Museum, which houses approximately 400 pieces of aircraft and space equipment on its grounds southeast of Tucson, is marking its 50th year of operation in 2026. The museum opened in 1976 and remains one of the largest aviation museums in the United States.
