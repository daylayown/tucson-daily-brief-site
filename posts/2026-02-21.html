# Tucson Daily Brief — February 21, 2026

Tucson Daily Brief — February 21, 2026

🏛️ Government

Tucson leaders resist cuts to housing, drug-use programs amid $27M deficit. City officials are navigating a projected $27 million shortfall this fiscal year, with Chief Financial Officer Anna Rosenberry presenting four options totaling $9.1 million in savings — a fraction of the gap. Mayor Regina Romero and Ward 6 Councilmember Miranda Schubert pushed back against proposals to suspend the P-CHIP housing investment program, citing the need for nonprofit funding at a time when federal safety nets are under pressure. A final budget decision has not been reached.

Arizona Luminaria

RTA Next election voter guide: What's at stake in the March vote. Pima County voters will decide on Propositions 418 & 419 in a mail-ballot election next month, determining how $2.67 billion in regional transportation funding is spent over the coming years. Roads in Marana, Oro Valley, and Tucson — many already cracked and delayed — hang in the balance. Crucially, the election does not raise taxes; it decides spending priorities for existing RTA revenues. Tucson Councilmember Miranda Schubert has broken with the RTA Next plan, calling it incompatible with the city's own transit vision.

Tucson Agenda

Pima County adopts policies restricting ICE enforcement on county property. Pima County supervisors approved two new policies Tuesday aimed at limiting federal immigration enforcement activity on county-owned land, including restrictions on raids and a prohibition on masked law enforcement. Supporters called the move a necessary statement of county values; Republican Supervisor Steve Christy dismissed it as "toothless." The Marana Town Council separately met with opposition group PRICE over a proposed ICE detention facility in the area.

AZPM / Tucson Agenda / Tucson Local Media (Explorer News)

Gov. Hobbs vetoes GOP bills imposing harsher SNAP penalties. Arizona Gov. Katie Hobbs on Saturday vetoed a package of Republican bills that would have imposed financial penalties on the state's Department of Economic Security if SNAP payment error rates don't drop below 3% by 2030. Hobbs cited the mounting costs already imposed by federal legislation, noting Arizona's DES faces up to $140 million in new SNAP-related expenses by 2028. "I invite you to join me in actually lowering costs for Arizona families," she wrote in her veto letter.

Arizona Mirror

Arizona bill would station immigration officers at polling places. State lawmakers have introduced legislation that would place immigration enforcement officers at Arizona polling locations, AZPM reported. The bill is part of a broader package of immigration-related legislation moving through the Legislature in recent weeks.

🚨 Public Safety

Pima County jail escapee caught in Mexico after three years on the run. A fugitive captured Friday in Mexico was deported to Tucson and booked into the Pima County jail Saturday, the U.S. Marshals Service announced. He faces charges including escape, domestic violence, aggravated assault, and kidnapping — all stemming from prior Pima County cases.

Arizona Daily Star

$102,500 reward now offered in Nancy Guthrie disappearance. 88-CRIME received an anonymous $100,000 donation this week, boosting the reward for information leading to the recovery of Nancy Guthrie — a Tucson woman who vanished — to $102,500. Earlier this week, the Pima County Sheriff's Department confirmed DNA from gloves found two miles from Guthrie's home did not match any national database profiles or DNA found at her property.

🎓 Education

UA eliminates cultural identity housing program, shifts to interdisciplinary model. The University of Arizona has dismantled a program that placed students from similar cultural backgrounds together in residence halls, replacing it with an interdisciplinary housing model. The shift comes amid broader reassessments of diversity-focused programs at universities nationally.

🏗️ Development & Business

New owner saves Tucson's oldest bookstore from closing. The Book Stop, located at 214 N. Fourth Ave. and long a fixture of Tucson's Fourth Avenue corridor, will remain open after new ownership stepped in. The venerable used bookstore had been facing closure before the deal was announced Saturday.

Arizona Daily Star

Brandylion Tea & Spirits opens today in downtown Tucson. Brandylion: Tea & Spirits held its grand opening Saturday at 200 N. Court Ave. at the Presidio San Agustín del Tucson Museum site. The venue occupies the space previously held by Dandelion Cafe and Bakery, which relocated across the street.

Oro Valley approves three-year phased stormwater fee hikes. Stormwater utility fees in Oro Valley will increase beginning July 1 under a phased three-year schedule approved by the town. Details on the amount of the increases were not included in published summaries.
