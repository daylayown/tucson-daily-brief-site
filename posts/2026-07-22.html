July 22, 2026

Wednesday

🚨 Public Safety

Downtown Tucson mass shooting suspect faces 19 charges; victim speaks out. The suspect in Sunday's downtown Tucson mass shooting, which injured nine people, remains unavailable for a court appearance and faces 19 charges with more possible, according to KOLD. A woman who says she was shot in the leg wrote publicly that she is "grateful to still be here," urging others to "hug your people a little tighter."

Arizona Daily Star, KOLD News 13

Downtown businesses review safety protocols and eye new technology after shooting. Several downtown Tucson businesses were forced to close Sunday during the police investigation and are monitoring the shooting's impact on foot traffic. By early August, many downtown establishments are scheduled to adopt technology that syncs customer identification data in real time as part of a coordinated safety initiative. The owner of Empire Pizza separately called for a focus on public safety measures over gun control in the aftermath.

Arizona Daily Star, Arizona Daily Star, KOLD News 13, KOLD News 13

Pima County Sheriff's Department searching for home invasion and assault suspect. Authorities are seeking a male suspect who allegedly entered a Tucson woman's home through an unlocked door, physically and sexually assaulted her, and attempted to burglarize the residence before fleeing, according to KVOA and KOLD.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13

🏛️ Government

Tucson City Council votes to support Tohono O'odham Nation's border wall lawsuit. At its Tuesday meeting, the Tucson City Council voted to formally support the Tohono O'odham Nation's federal lawsuit seeking to halt border wall construction on reservation lands. The council also addressed the weekend mass shooting at the session. A federal court hearing in Washington, D.C. on the tribe's lawsuit is scheduled for Wednesday, according to KVOA.

KGUN 9, KVOA News 4

Arizona primary results: Biggs wins GOP governor race; key statewide outcomes set November field. Rep. Andy Biggs won the Republican nomination for Arizona governor Tuesday, capturing more than 70% of the vote over chief rival Rep. David Schweikert, per the Associated Press call shortly after polls closed. Biggs will face Democratic incumbent Gov. Katie Hobbs in November. In other statewide results with Pima County implications: State Treasurer Kimberly Yee led incumbent Superintendent Tom Horne by roughly 32,000 votes in the GOP schools chief race; election attorney Alexander Kolodin won the Republican secretary of state nomination; and State Sen. Warren Petersen led in the attorney general GOP primary.

Arizona Luminaria, KGUN 9, KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13, Arizona Mirror, Arizona Mirror

Southern Arizona and Pima County primary results taking shape. Several Republicans running to represent Southern Arizona in Congress ran unopposed Tuesday, while a competitive legislative district race that includes part of Pima County drew voter interest, according to KVOA. Key Democratic primary contests were also being decided, including an open state senate seat in Legislative District 20 and Pima County local races. Initial results in the Sahuarita Town Council race showed two incumbents leading, with Proposition 420 — the town's general plan update — on track to pass nearly two to one, per the Green Valley News.

KVOA News 4, KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13, Green Valley News

Voucher reform ballot measure faces lawsuit to block voter signatures. The Goldwater Institute has filed suit seeking to disqualify tens of thousands of signatures supporting a citizen initiative — the "Protect Education, Accountability Now Act" — that would curtail Arizona's universal school voucher program and is backed by Save Our Schools Arizona. The initiative had submitted more than 420,000 signatures for the November ballot. A separate Arizona Daily Star report notes the lawsuit is an effort by universal voucher supporters to stop a public vote on the program.

Arizona Mirror, Arizona Daily Star

Pima County Board of Supervisors approves nearly $4 million in outside agency grants. The board voted 4-1 to approve the grants, with $233,780 directed specifically to nonprofits serving Green Valley, Sahuarita, Amado, and Arivaca, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

🏗️ Development & Business

Oro Valley Police Department moving toward long-needed new headquarters. The Oro Valley Police Department is converting a 50,500-square-foot former medical office building on Oracle Road into a new headquarters, addressing a need that Deputy Chief Curtiss Hicks says has existed "for two decades." The facility is expected to serve as a recruiting tool and may also house the department's dispatch operations, freeing space at the current town campus at 11000 N. La Canada, according to Tucson Local Media.

Tucson Local Media, Tucson Local Media, Tucson Local Media

Sahuarita advances La Villita Road extension project. The town of Sahuarita is moving forward with its first Regional Transportation Authority Next project: widening La Villita Road to two lanes in each direction and connecting Sahuarita Road with Nogales Highway to the south, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

$198,000 grant to fight buffelgrass at Catalina State Park. Two volunteer groups working on behalf of Catalina State Park have received a $198,000 grant from the Arizona Department of Forestry and Fire Management to remove invasive buffelgrass and protect saguaro populations, according to Tucson Local Media.
