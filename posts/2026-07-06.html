⛈️ Weather — Tucson

⚠️ Extreme Heat Warning in effect from 10 AM Tuesday through 8 PM Wednesday. NWS warns of dangerously hot conditions with highs of 107–111°F across the Tucson metro area, Tohono O'odham Nation, Western Pima County, and surrounding regions. Heat-related illness risk increases significantly during the event.

Today will be sunny with a high near 108°F; heat index values could reach 104°F. Winds west at 1–9 mph. Tonight will be clear with a low around 80°F. Tuesday brings another sunny, dangerous day with a high near 110°F and a heat index as high as 108°F. Wednesday stays near 110°F before a slight chance of afternoon thunderstorms arrives Thursday (20%), offering the first meaningful relief of the week. Residents should limit outdoor activity during peak afternoon hours, check on vulnerable neighbors, and seek air-conditioned spaces.

🚨 Public Safety

Woman killed in rollover crash at Davis-Monthan AFB. A 20-year-old woman died after being partially ejected from her vehicle in a rollover crash on Davis-Monthan Air Force Base in the early hours of Saturday, July 4. A teenage passenger sustained minor injuries. No additional details about the cause of the crash have been released.

Arizona Daily Star, KVOA, KOLD

Motorcyclist killed in Pima County crash near Valencia and Cardinal. A 34-year-old man died after his motorcycle collided with a pickup truck at the intersection of Cardinal Avenue and West Valencia Road on Saturday evening, according to the Pima County Sheriff's Department. His passenger was injured; deputies noted the rider was not wearing a helmet while his passenger was.

KGUN 9, KVOA, KOLD

Fireworks spark large brush fire on Tucson's south side. Tucson firefighters extinguished a large brush fire that ignited shortly after 9 p.m. Saturday on the south side of the city. Fireworks discovered in the street near the origin are believed to have caused the blaze, according to KOLD.

KVOA, KOLD

Wildfire in Dudleyville contained; forward progress stopped. Fire officials reported Sunday afternoon that forward progress had been stopped on the Sunnyvale Fire burning in the Pinal County river bottom near Dudleyville, a small community north of Tucson. Some residents had been ordered to evacuate as crews worked to contain the blaze.

KVOA, KOLD

🏛️ Government

Health leaders push back against closure of Indian Health Service Tucson office. Community and health advocates are mounting opposition to plans to close the Indian Health Service office in Tucson, according to KVOA. The office provides health care services to Native American populations in the region. No timeline for a final decision has been reported.

KVOA

Venezuelan woman released after nine months of wrongful ICE detention in Arizona. A Venezuelan woman was released from Immigration and Customs Enforcement custody in Arizona after being held for nine months in what authorities acknowledged was a wrongful detention, according to the Arizona Daily Star. The story also notes that the U.S. District Court in Arizona implemented policy changes in June aimed at reducing delays in releasing detained immigrants, amid soaring caseloads for immigration judges.

Arizona Daily Star

Tucson's "A" Mountain fireworks show held for the last time. The city's traditional Fourth of July fireworks display at "A" Mountain drew large crowds Saturday for what is expected to be its final showing, according to KVOA. The city plans to replace the annual fireworks show with a drone light display beginning next year.

KVOA

🎓 Education

University of Arizona launches semiconductor talent hub with Taiwan university. The University of Arizona and Taiwan's National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University are partnering to launch a semiconductor "Talent and Innovation Hub" set to debut in October, according to the Arizona Daily Star. The program will offer students from both institutions courses, internships, and cultural immersion experiences at campuses in Tucson and Hsinchu, Taiwan.
