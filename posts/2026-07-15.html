Daily Brief

July 15, 2026

Wednesday

🏛️ Government

Pima County Board of Supervisors postpones treasurer appointment. The Pima County Board of Supervisors did not select a new county treasurer at Tuesday's meeting, pushing the decision to a later date. No replacement was named for the vacant position.

KVOA News 4

Pima County backs Tohono O'odham Nation in border wall opposition. The Pima County Board of Supervisors approved a resolution opposing Trump administration plans to extend the border wall across Tohono O'odham Nation land in Arizona. County leaders aligned with tribal officials who have strongly objected to the proposed construction on their territory.

KVOA News 4, KVOA News 4

Amphitheater School District to ask voters to authorize property sales. Amphitheater Public Schools is placing a measure on the November 3 ballot seeking voter permission to sell, lease, or exchange land and buildings the district no longer needs. Separately, filing periods for Amphi and Marana school district governing board candidates have closed, with two competitive Amphi races set for the November general election.

Tucson Local Media / Explorer News

Oro Valley adopts new trails plan. The Oro Valley Town Council has approved a comprehensive plan to expand and improve the town's network of trails and outdoor pathways, according to KVOA. The plan is intended to boost access and connectivity across the community.

KVOA News 4

Oro Valley mayor's race takes shape. Two-term Town Councilmember and Vice Mayor Melanie Barrett will face former Pima County Sheriff Mark Napier in the race to lead Oro Valley, according to Tucson Local Media.

Tucson Local Media / Explorer News

Pima County still awaiting nearly $10 million in FEMA asylum-seeker reimbursements. Arizona's two U.S. senators have intervened on Pima County's behalf as FEMA has yet to release approximately $9.7 million in grants owed to the county for asylum-seeker services. The county has been waiting for the federal reimbursement for an extended period.

Green Valley News

U.S. House passes Sunshine Protection Act; Arizona delegation splits. The House voted 308-117 on Tuesday to make daylight saving time permanent nationwide. Arizona's congressional delegation voted 5-4 against the measure. The bill's fate in the Senate remains uncertain. Arizona already observes permanent standard time and does not observe daylight saving time.

Arizona Mirror, Cronkite News

🚨 Public Safety

Murder trial underway for man accused of killing Good Samaritan in Pima County. Opening arguments have concluded in the trial of Jack Daryl Upchurch, who faces charges including first-degree murder, armed robbery, aggravated robbery, kidnapping, and vehicle theft in connection with the killing of a Good Samaritan. Two co-defendants have entered plea deals and are cooperating with prosecutors.

KOLD News 13, KVOA News 4

Broadway Boulevard closed for emergency pavement repairs after monsoon damage; Pima County warns against moving barricades. A section of Broadway Boulevard was shut down following storm damage that left the pavement requiring urgent repairs. Pima County separately warned drivers not to move road closure barricades or attempt to drive through damaged pavement or floodwaters, noting that violations could carry legal consequences.

KVOA News 4, KGUN 9

Pima County teen's ATV stolen at gunpoint. The Pima County Sheriff's Department is investigating after a 14-year-old reported that his Yamaha ATV was taken from him at gunpoint Monday night, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

Pima County confirms cyclospora case amid multistate outbreak. Pima County health officials have confirmed one case of cyclosporiasis as a nationwide parasite outbreak reaches Arizona, with authorities expecting additional cases. Lettuce and salad greens have been identified as a potential source under investigation by state and federal officials.

KOLD News 13, Arizona Mirror

Tucson protesters rally against ICE-involved shootings. Demonstrators marched through downtown Tucson on Monday evening to protest the deaths of Joan Sebastian Guerrero and Lorenzo Salgado Araujo, who were killed by ICE agents in separate vehicle enforcement incidents. The Department of Homeland Security has since reportedly ordered ICE to cease vehicle-related enforcement stops, according to multiple media reports.

KGUN 9, Arizona Mirror

🏗️ Development & Business

Gibson Food Hall & Market opens quietly in downtown Tucson. The new Gibson Food Hall & Market has opened in the space formerly occupied by Johnny Gibson Downtown Market, featuring four restaurants, a grocery and convenience store, a bar, a coffee shop, and a speakeasy under one roof. Decorative mural panels that covered the windows during renovation are now being sold at auction.

Arizona Daily Star, Arizona Daily Star

Tucson gas prices spike nearly 20 cents overnight. Gas prices in Tucson jumped close to 20 cents in a single day, according to KVOA, marking the largest one-day increase in months. No cause for the spike was immediately identified.

KVOA News 4

Nearly $200,000 grant funds buffelgrass removal at Catalina State Park. Volunteers at Catalina State Park have received a grant of nearly $200,000 to remove invasive buffelgrass and protect native saguaro populations in the area, according to KVOA.

KVOA News 4

🎉 Community & Events

Sun Tran drivers ratify new three-year contract, strike averted. Tucson Sun Tran bus drivers voted to approve a new three-year labor agreement that includes major safety improvements, ending months of negotiations and avoiding a work stoppage that had threatened transit service.

Tucson Spotlight

Santa Cruz Humane Society postpones planned closure after $140,000 in donations. The donation-based Santa Cruz Humane Society, which serves the greater Green Valley area and had announced a fall closure due to insufficient funding, has pushed its closure date to the end of the year after a surge of more than $140,000 in community donations.

Green Valley News
