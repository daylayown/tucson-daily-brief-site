⛈️ Weather — Tucson

⚠️ Blowing Dust Advisory in effect from 4 PM to 11 PM MST today. NWS Tucson warns of visibility between one-quarter and one mile in blowing dust from thunderstorms late this afternoon and evening, with wind gusts exceeding 50 mph possible. Localized dense blowing dust may drop visibility below one-quarter mile at times, particularly along I-10 between Tucson and Phoenix. Hazardous driving conditions are expected.

Today: Mostly sunny with afternoon and evening thunderstorms likely; high near 103. Heat index up to 104°F. West-northwest winds 2–8 mph. 60% chance of precipitation. Tonight: Blowing dust and storms likely before 11 PM, then storm chances persist into early Sunday; low near 77.

Tomorrow (Sunday): Storms likely after 11 AM; high near 104, heat index up to 106°F. 70% chance of rain. The active monsoon pattern continues into next week, with storm chances of 70% Sunday night and Monday, and temperatures staying near or above 100°F through Tuesday.

🚨 Public Safety

Tucson police arrest suspect in 2004 sexual assault cold case. The Tucson Police Department has arrested 65-year-old John "Lincoln" Sperry in connection with the sexual assault of his then-14-year-old foster daughter — a case first reported more than two decades ago. Sperry faces two counts of sexual conduct with a minor.

KGUN 9, KOLD News 13

Erin Jones murder trials pushed to summer 2027. Trials for two men accused of killing University of Arizona student Erin Jones have been rescheduled for next summer, according to KVOA. No reason for the delay was immediately provided.

KVOA News 4

Tucson police warn of rising phone spoofing scams. Tucson Police Department is alerting residents to an increase in scams using fake caller ID numbers and spoofed text messages to deceive victims. Residents are urged to be cautious about unsolicited calls or texts requesting personal or financial information.

KVOA News 4

Sun Tran union vote set for Sunday as strike threat looms. Teamsters Local 104 is expected to vote Sunday on a contract offer from Sun Tran. If negotiations fail, the transit agency says riders could face limited service and longer wait times beginning Monday.

KOLD News 13

ICE detention measles outbreak draws calls for deportation delay. Rep. Adelita Grijalva, a Tucson Democrat, is calling for a pause in deportation proceedings for ICE detainees in Arizona affected by a measles outbreak at the Florence Detention Center, which has reported seven cases. The wife of one quarantined detainee, a Russian national, says it remains unconfirmed whether her husband has measles.

Arizona Daily Star, Tucson Spotlight

🏛️ Government

Pima County opens public portal for Prop 425 spending limit vote. The Pima County Elections Department has launched an online portal allowing residents to submit arguments for or against Proposition 425, a measure related to county spending limits, ahead of the July 21 primary election.

KVOA News 4

Local election officials brief community on voting security. Pima County agencies gathered Friday to outline joint efforts to ensure a safe, secure, and accessible July 21 primary, addressing community concerns about voter intimidation and interference. A federal judge this week also blocked an Arizona elections rule prohibiting "intimidating or harassing" clothing at polling places, a ruling that came fewer than two weeks before the primary.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13, Arizona Mirror

Eight candidates compete for South Tucson City Council seats. Eight candidates are vying for three seats on the South Tucson City Council in the July 21 primary election, according to Tucson Spotlight. Public safety, a budget deficit, and surveillance cameras are among the central issues in the race.

Tucson Spotlight

Pima County rolls out job training resources amid new SNAP work requirements. Pima County is offering job training and workforce assistance to residents affected by expanded federal work requirements for SNAP benefits. The county's outreach comes as some SNAP recipients face benefit delays, a situation the Pima Council on Aging says is also driving up demand for Meals on Wheels services.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13, KGUN 9

Arizona Corporation Commission approves new TEP transmission line for Pima and Santa Cruz counties. The Arizona Corporation Commission voted July 8 to approve a Tucson Electric Power transmission line project serving Pima and Santa Cruz counties, among 16 matters acted upon at the commission's open meeting.

KVOA News 4

ESA voucher limit initiative likely heading to November ballot. A ballot initiative that would place new restrictions on Arizona's universal Education Savings Account voucher program appears likely to qualify for the November ballot, according to Nogales International. The measure would cap or limit ESA eligibility; opponents have raised concerns about its scope.

🏗️ Development & Business

Mount Lemmon water restrictions pressure short-term rental operators. Stage 3 water restrictions on Mount Lemmon are straining short-term rental property owners during the peak summer tourism season, according to KOLD. The restrictions limit water use at a time when visitor demand is high.

KOLD News 13

Habitat for Humanity Tucson accepting applications for veteran and 55+ housing community. Habitat for Humanity Tucson is now accepting applications for Casitas on Park, a new affordable housing development restricted to adults 55 and older and veterans. Additional details on eligibility and the application process are available through the organization.

KGUN 9

Sahuarita sees new business activity, including first movie theater tied to incentive program. Sahuarita is reporting progress on several new business openings, including a movie theater that became the first project to utilize the town's business incentive program, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News & Sahuarita Sun

🎉 Community & Events

U of A program offers free liver screenings to Southern Arizona Latinos. The University of Arizona's Nosotros program is providing free liver scans to Southern Arizona's Latino community, targeting fatty liver disease that disproportionately affects people of Mexican origin. The screenings are aimed at early detection of a condition that often goes undiagnosed.

Tucson Spotlight

Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum names rescued mountain lion cubs. The Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum has revealed the names of its two rescued mountain lion cubs — Moonbead and Pretzel — chosen through a partnership that gave children facing serious illnesses the opportunity to name the animals.
