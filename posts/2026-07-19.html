July 19, 2026

Sunday

🚨 Public Safety

Teen motorcyclist killed in midtown Tucson crash. Jaycob Christopher Nieto, 19, died early Saturday morning after a collision at East Speedway Boulevard and North Richey Boulevard just after midnight, according to the Tucson Police Department. Details on the cause of the crash have not been released.

Arizona Daily Star, KGUN 9, KVOA, KOLD

Three bodies recovered from wash near Nogales over two days. Search and rescue crews pulled two additional bodies from a monsoon-swollen wash north of Nogales on Saturday, a day after the first body was recovered from the same location, authorities said. Separately, the body of a missing Tucson man was found in the Santa Cruz River just south of Tucson.

Arizona Daily Star, KVOA, KOLD, Green Valley News, Nogales International

Measles case confirmed in Tucson; Pima County identifies exposure locations. Pima County health officials are investigating a confirmed measles case involving a Tucson resident who recently returned from international travel, according to the Pima County Health Department. Authorities have listed locations of possible public exposure and are urging residents who may have been at those sites to monitor for symptoms.

Arizona Daily Star, KVOA, KOLD

Pregnant detainee airlifted to Tucson hospital after alleged assault by Border Patrol agent. Two pregnant women in immigration custody in Arizona were hospitalized in separate incidents this month. In one case, a woman was helicoptered to a Tucson hospital after alleging she was struck by a Border Patrol agent, according to the Arizona Daily Star. The incidents are under review.

Arizona Daily Star

Tucson protesters rally against ICE shooting deaths. Community members and members of the Party for Socialism and Liberation gathered in downtown Tucson on Saturday to demonstrate against the deaths of Lorenzo Salgado Araujo and Joan Sebastian Guerrero, two men killed by ICE officers in incidents that have prompted protests nationally.

KGUN 9

Murder trial underway for man accused of killing good Samaritan in Pima County. Trial is continuing for Jack Daryl Upchurch, who faces charges including first-degree murder, armed robbery, aggravated robbery, kidnapping, vehicle theft, third-degree burglary, and evidence tampering in connection with the killing of a good Samaritan in Pima County, according to KOLD.

KOLD

Monsoon runoff buckles East Broadway near Freeman Wash; road remains closed. Monsoon runoff damaged East Broadway at South Freeman Road on Tucson's east side, prompting a road closure that some drivers have been bypassing despite barricades. Officials warn that ignoring closures could result in citations and poses safety risks.

KOLD

🏛️ Government

Emergency voting open in Pima County ahead of 2026 Primary Election. Pima County voters can cast ballots through emergency voting options beginning this weekend ahead of the 2026 Primary Election, according to KVOA. Additional details on locations and hours were available through the county elections office.

KVOA

Federal judge appoints receiver to oversee Arizona prison healthcare. A federal judge on Friday appointed Ohio's former prisons chief as a receiver with sweeping authority over Arizona's prison healthcare system, granting her sole power to determine what steps the state must take to end constitutional violations stemming from substandard inmate medical care, according to the Arizona Mirror. The ruling also sets the stage for nullifying two state laws that experts have challenged.

Arizona Mirror

🎓 Education

Sahuarita and Walden Grove high school libraries renovated ahead of fall semester. Students returning to Sahuarita High School and Walden Grove High School this fall will find modernized library spaces following upgrades completed over the summer, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

University of Arizona's KEYS Research Program marks 20th anniversary. The U of A's KEYS Research Program, which connects high school students with university researchers, celebrated its 20th anniversary Friday, according to KOLD.

KOLD
