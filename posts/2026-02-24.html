🏛️ Government

TUSD board votes today on $10M in cuts, job eliminations amid looming deficit. The Tucson Unified School District board is set to vote Tuesday on a package of cuts from its non-desegregation maintenance and operations budget totaling $10 million for the upcoming school year. Superintendent Dr. Gabriel Trujillo is recommending 7% budget cuts across every administrative department — including positions within his own office — and the closure of the district's customer service center, eliminating 17 full-time positions. The moves are driven by declining enrollment and a projected $27 million deficit by fiscal year 2030.

KGUN 9

Tucson leaders push back on housing and drug program cuts amid $27M city deficit. Facing a $27 million shortfall in the current fiscal year, Tucson's mayor and council reaffirmed their commitment to nonprofit housing programs and public drug use prevention initiatives during a Feb. 18 study session. City CFO Anna Rosenberry presented four options totaling $9.1 million in savings — only a fraction of the deficit — with options including foregoing general fund contributions to the city's affordable housing investment plan. Council members pushed back on the deeper cuts.

Arizona Luminaria

RTA Next election next month will shape regional roads and transit for years. Voters across the Tucson region will decide in March on the $2.67 billion RTA Next transportation plan, which determines which roads get repaired and how public transit is funded in coming years. The measure does not raise taxes but draws on an existing regional sales tax. Major projects for Marana include long-delayed improvements to Silverbell and Tangerine roads. The vote also carries significant consequences for Sun Tran bus service.

Tucson Agenda

Pima County passes anti-ICE resolution restricting federal agents on county property. The Pima County Board of Supervisors approved a resolution placing limits on ICE activity on county-owned property, prohibiting unmasked law enforcement during raids and restricting cooperation with federal immigration detainer requests. Republican Supervisor Christy called it "toothless," while supporters framed it as a signal to federal agents operating in Southern Arizona.

Tucson Agenda

Arizona Senate sends trans bathroom ban to voters, bypassing governor. The Arizona Senate on Monday approved Senate Concurrent Resolution 1006, which would place a transgender bathroom and pronoun policy ban on the November ballot. The measure would require public schools to police restrooms and locker rooms based on biological sex, prohibit teachers from using students' preferred pronouns, and apply to overnight accommodations on school trips. Republican lawmakers are routing the measure directly to voters after years of vetoes from Gov. Katie Hobbs.

Arizona Mirror

UA dismantles cultural housing communities, shifts to interdisciplinary model. A University of Arizona program that grouped students from similar cultural backgrounds together in residence halls will no longer center on cultural identity. The university is rebranding the communities with an interdisciplinary academic focus, ending dedicated housing for groups previously organized around shared heritage or background.

🚨 Public Safety

Sheriff calls prior-photo claim about Guthrie suspect "purely speculative." Pima County Sheriff Chris Nanos pushed back Monday on media reports — citing anonymous sources — that a masked man seen in surveillance footage at Nancy Guthrie's door had previously been photographed at the same address. Nanos urged caution about the unverified claim as investigators continue working the case. Separately, Pima County officials earlier this week converted the residential street near Guthrie's home to one-way traffic to manage three weeks of sustained media presence outside the property.

🏗️ Development & Business

Tucson opens public comment period on data center regulations. As large-scale data centers increasingly target Arizona for expansion, the City of Tucson launched its first public meetings on proposed rules that would govern where and how such facilities could operate. The regulations would address impacts on power infrastructure, water use, and land use zoning. Residents can submit feedback during the ongoing comment period.

AZPM

Oro Valley stormwater fees rising July 1 in three-year phased increase. Stormwater utility fees in Oro Valley will begin increasing effective July 1 under a three-year phased rate hike approved by the town. The increases are intended to fund infrastructure maintenance and flood control improvements.

🎉 Community & Events

Wildflower season arriving early across Southern Arizona. A warmer-than-average winter has triggered early blooms at Picacho Peak and Lost Dutchman State Park, with forecasters saying conditions are favorable for a "superbloom" this spring. This Is Tucson reports the early season offers unusually good viewing opportunities through March for residents willing to make the short drive north.

Hotdogs 501 brings Sonoran-style hot dogs to Main Avenue. A family-owned hot dog restaurant built almost entirely by its owners — including hand-poured concrete, custom-welded tables, and owner-done electrical work — has opened at the corner of Speedway and Main Avenue. Hotdogs 501 features Sonoran-style dogs and launched by handing out free samples to passing drivers during rush hour.
