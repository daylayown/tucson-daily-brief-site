July 17, 2026

Friday

🚨 Public Safety

Two bodies reported in Nogales wash after monsoon flooding. Two bodies were spotted floating down a wash in Nogales during a heavy downpour Thursday evening and had not been recovered as of 10 p.m., according to reports. Santa Cruz County Sheriff's Office crews were searching the area near Rio Rico. Authorities have not yet confirmed identities or cause of death.

Nogales International, KVOA, KOLD

Marana barrel racer killed in hit-and-run. Kyrsten Sovereign, 23, of Marana, was killed July 11 in a hit-and-run crash while traveling to a barrel racing event, according to the Arizona Daily Star. No further details on suspect or vehicle have been reported.

Arizona Daily Star

Monsoon storm devastates Tucson renter's home; family uninsured. A Tucson family faces costly repairs after 80 mph winds tore through their home last weekend, according to KOLD. As a renter, the family had no insurance to cover the damage.

KOLD

Pima County cyclospora infections rise to five cases. Pima County health officials have confirmed five reported cases of cyclospora, an intestinal infection whose symptoms include gas, cramping, bloating, and watery diarrhea, according to the Arizona Daily Star. Officials have not publicly identified a common source.

Arizona Daily Star

Pima County Sheriff's Department holds annual active shooter preparedness workshop. The PCSD hosted its annual Active School Shooter Preparedness Workshop, teaching the "Run, Hide, Fight" protocol through classroom instruction and realistic scenarios as the new school year approaches.

KVOA, KOLD

🏛️ Government

Judge allows Arizona GOP lawmakers to intervene in Prop. 314 border law defense. A federal judge has granted Arizona's top Republican legislative leaders permission to help defend Proposition 314 — the voter-approved border enforcement law — in federal court. The ACLU is challenging the measure's constitutionality, and the move comes amid questions over Attorney General Kris Mayes' role in the defense.

Arizona Daily Star

Trailer park firm sued by AG Mayes emerges as top donor to PAC backing her opponent. BoaVida Group — a trailer park management company facing a lawsuit from Democratic Attorney General Kris Mayes over deadly power outages at a Tucson park — is the largest donor to a PAC supporting GOP candidate Warren Petersen in the attorney general race, according to the Arizona Mirror. Mayes filed the lawsuit after outages during a summer heat wave affected residents at Tucson's Redwood park.

Arizona Mirror

Santa Cruz County mail-in ballot issue reported ahead of primary. The Santa Cruz County Recorder's Office acknowledged it is aware of several dozen instances in which voters who requested mail-in primary ballots have not received them, Recorder Anita Moreno said Thursday. Affected voters were advised to contact the office for alternatives.

Nogales International

🏗️ Development & Business

Gibson Food Hall & Market opens in downtown Tucson after nearly two years of renovations. The new Gibson Food Hall & Market has quietly opened in downtown Tucson, replacing the former Johnny Gibson Downtown Market. The revamped space features four restaurants, a grocery and convenience store, a bar, and a coffee shop. A speakeasy called the Pearl Lounge is expected to open in mid-August during the official grand opening.

Arizona Daily Star, KOLD

ADOT project to widen I-19 ramps and add bridge near Tucson. Drivers on Tucson's southwest side should prepare for a multi-year construction project at the I-19 and Irvington Road interchange, according to KVOA. The Arizona Department of Transportation project will widen ramps and add a new bridge, with impacts to commutes expected over the next couple of years.

KVOA

Tucson's FoodCycle at Home program adds two new drop-off sites. The city's FoodCycle at Home program, which turns food scraps into compost and mulch, expanded last week with the addition of two new collection sites, bringing the total to 18 locations across Tucson, according to KGUN9.

KGUN9
