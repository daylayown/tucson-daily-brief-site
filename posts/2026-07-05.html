⛈️ Weather — Tucson

⚠️ Extreme Heat Watch in effect from Tuesday morning through Wednesday evening (July 8, 8 PM MST). NWS Tucson warns of dangerously hot conditions with temperatures of 107–111°F across the Tucson metro area, Western Pima County, Tohono O'odham Nation, and surrounding regions. Heat-related illness risk increases significantly during these events; residents are urged to limit outdoor exposure and check on vulnerable neighbors.

Today (Sunday): Mostly sunny, high near 106°F. Heat index values as high as 101°F. West wind 1–8 mph. Tonight: Partly cloudy, low around 78°F. Heat index values as high as 100°F. Monday: Sunny, high near 108°F; heat index up to 104°F. Low around 80°F. Outlook: Temperatures climb to near 110°F Tuesday and Wednesday, with a slight chance of afternoon thunderstorms Wednesday (20%). The pattern offers little relief until monsoon moisture increases later next week.

🚨 Public Safety

Fatal crash on Davis-Monthan Air Force Base kills 20-year-old woman. A 20-year-old woman died early Saturday morning after being partially ejected from her vehicle during a rollover crash on the 8700 block of East Yuma Street inside Davis-Monthan Air Force Base. A second occupant, a teenager, sustained minor injuries. The incident is under investigation.

KGUN 9, KVOA, KOLD

Two people killed in I-10 crash near Vail. A Friday night collision on Interstate 10 near Vail killed two people and shut down all eastbound lanes for several hours overnight. No additional details on the cause have been released.

KVOA, KOLD

Venezuelan woman released after 9 months of wrongful ICE detention in Arizona. A Venezuelan woman held in ICE custody in Arizona for nine months was released after being found to have been wrongfully detained, according to the Arizona Daily Star. The case comes as the U.S. District Court in Arizona implemented policy changes in June aimed at reducing delays in releasing detained immigrants, citing soaring caseloads for immigration judges.

Arizona Daily Star

Three arrested after security guard hospitalized in midtown assault. Three men were arrested after Tucson Police say a security guard was attacked while patrolling a midtown gas station. The guard was hospitalized; the incident occurred in the early morning hours of July 3, according to KVOA.

KVOA

Missing Tucson man found safe. Tucson Police had issued a search for a missing and vulnerable 22-year-old man near Campbell Avenue on July 4; he was subsequently located, according to KOLD.

KVOA, KOLD

🏛️ Government

Sahuarita launches FAQ ahead of July 21 general plan vote amid data center concerns. Sahuarita town staff released a frequently-asked-questions document Saturday about the 2026 General Plan ahead of the Proposition 420 vote scheduled for July 21, according to the Green Valley News. The move comes amid growing resident confusion about whether the plan could facilitate data center development in the town.

Green Valley News

Tucson's A Mountain fireworks show ends; drone show planned for 2025. The City of Tucson's annual Fourth of July fireworks display at "A" Mountain drew thousands of spectators Saturday night in what officials have confirmed is its final edition. The city announced it will transition to a drone show for future Independence Day celebrations.

KGUN 9, KVOA, KOLD

🎉 Community & Events

Mission Garden earns National Register of Historic Places designation. Mission Garden, the agricultural living history museum at the base of "A" Mountain, has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places, according to the Arizona Daily Star. The site preserves thousands of years of farming history in the Tucson Basin.

Arizona Daily Star

Tucson teen wins best actress at national high school musical theatre awards. Tucson native Samia Posadas won the best actress award at the Jimmy Awards — the National High School Musical Theatre Awards — held in New York, according to the Arizona Daily Star. Posadas was among eight finalists and said she was not expecting to hear her name called.

Arizona Daily Star

World Cup fever boosting business at Tucson restaurants and bars. Tucson restaurants and bars are reporting surging crowds and sales as the FIFA World Cup draws viewers, particularly when the Mexican national team plays, according to KVOA.

KVOA
