🏛️ Government

State lawmaker calls for probe of Tucson's ICE ban. A Republican state lawmaker has asked Attorney General Kris Mayes to investigate Tucson and other Arizona cities that bar Immigration and Customs Enforcement from operating on city property, contending the policy is illegal under state law. The demand targets Tucson's longstanding ordinance restricting ICE access to city facilities, which has drawn renewed scrutiny amid the Trump administration's intensified immigration enforcement campaign.

Arizona Daily Star

Tucson holds public meetings on proposed rate hikes for water, trash and other services. The City of Tucson is hosting public meetings this week to gather input on proposed rate increases for water, trash collection, and other city services. Primary elections are also underway, and Tucson Sentinel journalists are scheduled to appear on local radio to discuss upcoming races.

Tucson Sentinel

Rep. Eli Crane faces bipartisan backlash after welcoming Russian Duma members. Rep. Eli Crane (R-Oro Valley) is drawing criticism from both parties after co-hosting a meeting that brought five Russian State Duma members together with five U.S. House members. The visit has raised concerns about diplomatic optics at a time of ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine.

Tucson Sentinel

Southern Arizona's 2026 election fields take shape. The Tucson Sentinel has published a comprehensive roundup of filing-period results for Southern Arizona's midterm races. In Congressional District 6 — expected to be one of the most-watched House races in the country — Republican Rep. Juan Ciscomani faces Democrat JoAnna Mendoza in his bid for a third term. Appointed Pima County Supervisor Andrés Cano is running unopposed in a special election, while contested council races are set in Oro Valley and Marana.

Tucson Sentinel, Tucson Sentinel, Tucson Local Media

Oro Valley proposes $1.86/month increase to base water rate. Oro Valley households could see a $1.86 increase in their monthly base water rate charge beginning in early July, according to a proposal the town is reviewing. Separately, the town's water utility is reporting a decline in high-volume residential water users.

Tucson Local Media

🏗️ Development & Business

TEP and AEPC bring new Cochise County solar farms online, powering 90,000+ homes. Tucson Electric Power and Arizona Electric Power Cooperative have completed new solar installations in Cochise County that collectively supply renewable energy to more than 90,000 homes across Arizona. TEP's Babacomari Solar facility alone features more than 350,000 panels and connects to the company's existing transmission corridor between Fort Huachuca and the Tucson area.

KGUN 9

Pima Air and Space Museum marks 50 years. The Pima Air and Space Museum, one of the largest aviation and aerospace museums in the world, is celebrating its 50th anniversary in 2026. The museum, located south of Tucson, has welcomed visitors from around the world to its collection of roughly 400 aircraft since opening in 1976.

Inside Tucson Business

🎉 Community & Events

Tucson leaders convene extreme heat summit; Donna Liggins center to become first resilience hub. Mayor Regina Romero, Congresswoman Adelita Grijalva, and community advocates gathered at the Donna Liggins Recreation Center to discuss strategies for reducing extreme heat impacts on vulnerable residents. Mayor Romero announced the center near Grant and Stone will be upgraded into Tucson's first "resilience hub," where residents will be able to shelter for up to three days during extreme heat events.

KGUN 9

UA Wildcats' Final Four run carries deep meaning for Tucson. The University of Arizona men's basketball team is in the Final Four, set to play Saturday in Indianapolis — a milestone that has energized the city. For the owner of Rusty's Family Restaurant and Sports Grille on Tucson's Westside, the run carries added significance: she is honoring her late son, a longtime UA superfan who died of brain cancer last March.
