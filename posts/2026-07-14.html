July 14, 2026

Tuesday

🏛️ Government

Pima County steps in to fund jail GED program after sheriff canceled contract. Pima County is funding a decades-old GED program at the county jail after Sheriff Chris Nanos canceled its contract with Pima Community College, citing cost and unproven results. The county's intervention keeps the program operational for incarcerated residents pursuing their high school equivalency credential.

Tucson Spotlight

Pima County jail recidivism drops, but overdose deaths after release remain a crisis. Pima County is reporting a decline in recidivism among jail releases, though officials say overdose deaths occurring shortly after release continue to represent a significant public health challenge.

KOLD News 13

Pima County voters get new early ballot drop-off option ahead of July 21 election. Pima County voters can drop off early ballots at any vote center on July 21, with sufficient ID allowing them to bypass the signature verification process and speed up Election Day tabulation, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News & Sahuarita Sun

ACLU seeks federal court order to block Arizona's Prop. 314 border enforcement law. An immigrant rights legal group has filed suit asking a federal court to block Arizona's Proposition 314, which would empower local police and state judges to arrest and deport people who cross the southern border without authorization. The law, approved by 61% of voters in 2024, is set to take effect in four days.

Arizona Mirror

Republican Anthony Dunham enters race for open LD17 Arizona Senate seat. Republican Anthony Dunham has announced a run for the open Legislative District 17 Arizona Senate seat, according to Tucson Spotlight. He faces Christopher King in the primary, running on a platform centered on taxes, border security, and school choice.

Tucson Spotlight

Former Sahuarita town employee receives probation for theft. A former Town of Sahuarita employee who pleaded guilty in June to theft was sentenced Monday to three years of probation and ordered to complete 100 hours of community service.

Green Valley News & Sahuarita Sun

🚨 Public Safety

PCSD warns of QR code scam exploiting Nancy Guthrie investigation. The Pima County Sheriff's Department is warning the public about fraudulent QR codes circulating online that solicit money under the guise of the ongoing Nancy Guthrie case. Residents are urged not to scan or respond to unsolicited requests tied to the investigation.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13

Monsoon storms flood Tucson streets, prompt multiple vehicle rescues. Monsoon storms swept through Tucson on Monday, flooding roadways and prompting the Tucson Fire Department to respond to at least three calls involving vehicles stranded in high water. Northside neighborhoods also saw downed trees, split utility poles, and a tree fall on a home, with additional rain returning to the Sabino Canyon area Monday evening.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13

One person suffers life-threatening injuries near I-10 and 22nd Street. One person was transported with life-threatening injuries following an incident on Interstate 10 near 22nd Street in Tucson on Monday afternoon, according to KOLD News 13. No further details on circumstances were available.

KOLD News 13

Tucson teen missing for more than four months; public asked for help. The National Center for Missing & Exploited Children is asking for the public's assistance in locating 16-year-old Aubrey Nichols, a Tucson teenager who has been missing for more than four months, according to KVOA News 4. No further details on last known whereabouts were provided.

KVOA News 4

🎓 Education

University of Arizona set to break ground on new first-year student dormitory. The University of Arizona is scheduled to break ground on a new residence hall for first-year students, according to KVOA News 4. Additional details on timeline and capacity were not immediately available.

KVOA News 4

TUSD holding bus driver hiring event Wednesday. Tucson Unified School District is hosting a hiring event on Wednesday, July 15, for both CDL and non-CDL bus drivers, according to KOLD News 13.

KOLD News 13

🏗️ Development & Business

Santa Rita Park to receive $8 million renovation, expected done by fall. Tucson's Santa Rita Park is undergoing an $8 million makeover that is expected to be completed by fall, according to the Arizona Daily Star. Additional details on specific improvements were not included in available reporting.

Arizona Daily Star

Tucson's Piñata Factory closes North Stone Avenue storefront after 17 years. The Piñata Factory, which has handcrafted custom piñatas in Tucson since 2009, has closed its North Stone Avenue storefront. The owner cited the impact of online shopping on in-person retail as a key factor in the decision, according to Tucson Spotlight.

Tucson Spotlight

Santa Cruz Humane Society postpones closure after $140,000 donation surge. The donation-based Santa Cruz Humane Society, which had announced a fall closure last month due to insufficient funds, has extended its closure date to the end of the year after receiving more than $140,000 in community donations.
