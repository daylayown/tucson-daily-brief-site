July 9, 2026

Thursday

⛈️ Weather — Tucson

⚠️ Extreme Heat Warning in effect until 8 PM MST tonight. NWS Tucson warns of dangerously hot conditions with temperatures of 107–112°F across the Tucson Metro Area, Western Pima County, and the Tohono O'odham Nation. Heat-related illness risk is significantly elevated.

Today will be sunny with a high near 109°F; southeast winds shift westerly at 1–12 mph. A slight chance of evening thunderstorms (20%) arrives after 5 p.m. Tonight stays warm — low around 79°F — with a lingering storm chance before 11 p.m. Friday brings another hot day near 106°F with afternoon storm chances increasing to 30%. The weekend offers some relief: highs around 104°F Saturday and Sunday, with monsoon storm chances climbing to 40–60% by Sunday afternoon as moisture pushes north.

🏛️ Government

Tucson lands $25M federal grant for Rillito River Bridge reconstruction. The city of Tucson has been awarded a $25 million federal grant to replace the Rillito River bridge and reconstruct approximately one mile of North 1st Avenue between Wetmore Road and River Road, according to KVOA.

KVOA News 4

Irvington Road / I-19 interchange construction set to begin this month. A $53.4 million reconstruction project at the Irvington Road and Interstate 19 interchange is scheduled to begin in July, according to KOLD. The project is expected to take approximately 20 months to complete.

KOLD News 13

Pima County releases updated Interstate 11 recommendations. Pima County has issued updated recommendations on the proposed Interstate 11 corridor, cautioning that a route through Avra Valley could carry environmental impacts beyond what current data reflects, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Oro Valley voters head to the polls July 21 to elect mayor, council. Oro Valley voters will choose a new mayor and three council members, with candidates staking out positions on surveillance cameras, town growth, the Vistoso Trails pond restoration, and town finances. Candidates for several seats have declined PAC and developer contributions. Multiple outlets are previewing the race ahead of the July 21 election.

Tucson Spotlight, Tucson Local Media

Oro Valley Town Council adopts 2.5% use tax on out-of-state business purchases. For the second time, the Oro Valley Town Council has voted to adopt a first-ever 2.5% use tax on certain out-of-state purchases made by local businesses, according to Tucson Local Media.

Tucson Local Media

Arizona's transgender school sports ban challenge collapses. A legal challenge to Arizona's ban on transgender athletes in school sports is set to end after the lone teen plaintiff withdrew from the case, according to the Arizona Mirror. The lawsuit had been in litigation for several years.

Arizona Mirror

🚨 Public Safety

Measles outbreak confirmed at ICE detention center near Tucson region. Seven active measles cases have been confirmed at the ICE Florence Detention Center in Arizona, a Department of Homeland Security spokesperson said Tuesday, according to the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

Pima County Attorney highlights challenges in prosecuting sexual assault cold cases. DNA testing of a backlog of rape kits has yielded 14 convictions in Pima County, with additional cases still pending, according to KOLD. The county attorney outlined the legal and evidentiary hurdles involved in prosecuting cases from decades prior.

KOLD News 13

Tucson Police identify suspect in 1995 cold case homicide. Tucson Police announced they have identified a suspect in a cold case dating to 1995, more than 30 years after the original crime, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Armed robbers steal $40,000 in tactical gear and firearms from east-side optics store. Two armed individuals broke into Riton Optics on Tucson's east side on the morning of June 21, stealing nearly $40,000 worth of tactical gear, including rifle scopes, compressors, and firearms, according to KGUN9.

KGUN 9

Former church treasurer accused of stealing more than $400,000. A former treasurer is facing charges of stealing more than $400,000 from a Tucson-area church, according to KOLD. Additional details were not immediately available.

KOLD News 13

🎓 Education

TUSD chronic absenteeism rate among worst in Arizona. Nearly half of students in Tucson Unified School District are chronically absent, and state data indicates the problem is worsening, according to KOLD. The trend mirrors a national issue; at least six states have enacted new laws this year aimed at reducing chronic absenteeism.

KOLD News 13

U of A places two more fraternities on probation. The University of Arizona has placed Sigma Phi Epsilon and Phi Delta Theta on probation following investigations that began this spring, according to KVOA.

KVOA News 4

Baboquivari school district builds teacher housing to address vacancy crisis. The Baboquivari Unified School District, a rural district in western Pima County, is using federal funding to construct affordable housing aimed at attracting and retaining teachers, as Arizona faces more than 4,000 teaching vacancies statewide, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

🏗️ Development & Business

Coronado National Forest adds nearly 700 acres in the Catalina Mountains. Almost 700 acres of grasslands and oak woodland near Oracle — once proposed for residential development — have been added to the Coronado National Forest in the Catalina Mountains, according to the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

Federal approval granted for critical-minerals mine in Patagonia jaguar habitat. The U.S. Forest Service has given final approval to a proposed mine in southern Arizona that would extract up to five critical minerals. The mine site sits within critical habitat for jaguars and Mexican spotted owls, according to AZ Luminaria, citing Inside Climate News.

Arizona Luminaria

Tucson restaurant closes amid summer slowdown. Dante's Fire on East Grant Road will hold a final whiskey dinner Sunday before permanently closing July 19, according to the Arizona Daily Star. It is the third Tucson restaurant to close since mid-June.

Arizona Daily Star

🎉 Community & Events

Tucson breaks ground on long-awaited downtown skate park. A public groundbreaking ceremony for the Cushing Street Skate Park was held June 30, following more than a decade of planning, according to Tucson Local Media. Construction is now underway.

Tucson Local Media

Rep. Grijalva hosts walk-in passport fair today in Tucson. U.S. Rep. Adelita Grijalva is scheduled to host a Passport Fair today, July 9, from 3–7 p.m. at El Pueblo Center, offering walk-in assistance for residents applying for or renewing U.S. passports, according to KVOA.
