Daily Brief

July 21, 2026

Tuesday

🚨 Public Safety

Suspect identified in downtown Tucson mass shooting; 10 injured. The suspect in Sunday morning's downtown Tucson mass shooting has been identified as 21-year-old David Leroy French, who faces 19 charges with the possibility of more, according to authorities. Several victims have been released from the hospital while others continue to recover. TPD Chief Monica Prieto praised the emergency response and said officers will maintain an increased downtown presence.

KOLD News 13, KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13

Measles exposure sites identified in Pima County. Pima County health officials identified measles exposure locations in Tucson and Oro Valley after a vaccinated resident was diagnosed with measles following international travel, according to KGUN 9. Residents who may have visited the identified sites are urged to check whether they were exposed.

KGUN 9

Homicide investigated near Oracle and Glenn. Tucson Police are investigating a homicide that occurred just after midnight Sunday, July 19, near the intersection of Oracle Road and Glenn Street, according to KOLD News 13.

KOLD News 13

Mt. Lemmon community under mandatory water restrictions. Summer Haven is under a mandatory Stage 3 water curtailment after well production dropped to as little as 25% of normal demand. Residents face fines and meter lockouts for noncompliance, according to Tucson Spotlight.

Tucson Spotlight

🏛️ Government

2026 Primary Election underway in Pima County today. Polls are open across Pima County for the 2026 Primary Election, with voters able to cast ballots at any Vote Center in the county. High-profile local races include contests for South Tucson City Council, Oro Valley Town Council, Arizona State Senate District 20, and Arizona House District 19, among others. The race for Arizona Superintendent of Public Instruction has become the most expensive such primary in decades, according to Cronkite News.

KVOA News 4, KOLD News 13, Cronkite News

Lawsuit seeks to block school voucher reform measure from November ballot. The Goldwater Institute filed suit to remove the "Protect Education, Accountability Now Act" from the November ballot, seeking to disqualify tens of thousands of signatures gathered by Save Our Schools Arizona, which submitted more than 420,000 signatures. The initiative would rein in Arizona's universal school voucher program.

Arizona Mirror, Arizona Daily Star

Pima County Board of Supervisors approves nearly $4 million in outside agency grants. The board voted 4-1 to approve the grants, with $233,780 directed to nonprofits serving Green Valley, Sahuarita, Amado, and Arivaca communities, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

Arizona AG asks court not to block Proposition 314. Arizona Attorney General Kris Mayes urged a federal judge to reject an immediate injunction against Proposition 314, the state's new border immigration law, arguing no one has yet been arrested or harmed under it, according to the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

🎓 Education

UA whistleblower lawsuit heads toward possible jury trial ruling. A Pima County Superior Court judge is expected to rule soon on whether a lawsuit filed by a former University of Arizona employee can proceed to trial, according to AZ Luminaria. The case provides a window into alleged dysfunction within UA's College of Education in the period preceding the university's widely reported financial crisis.

AZ Luminaria

TUSD expands free back-to-school supply giveaways. Tucson Unified School District has expanded its free backpack and school supply giveaway program ahead of the new school year, with supplies including notebooks, folders, pencils, and highlighters available for students, according to AZ Luminaria.

AZ Luminaria

🏗️ Development & Business

Downtown Tucson businesses weigh impact of mass shooting on foot traffic. Several businesses forced to close Sunday during the police investigation say the disruption affected operations and are monitoring for any lasting effect on downtown activity and customer perception, according to the Arizona Daily Star and KOLD News 13.

Arizona Daily Star, KOLD News 13

Downtown Marana entertainment district clears final legal hurdle. The long-planned Downtown Marana entertainment district has received final legal clearance and will now advance toward construction, according to KVOA News 4.

KVOA News 4

New TPD pilot program to target 50 people most frequently convicted of low-level offenses. The Tucson Police Department is launching a pilot program that would identify the 50 individuals most often convicted of trespassing, loitering, and drug paraphernalia possession, offering them additional services. Those who refuse or reoffend could face enhanced criminal penalties, according to AZ Luminaria.

AZ Luminaria

Two MQ-9 Reaper drones temporarily relocating to Davis-Monthan AFB. Residents near Davis-Monthan Air Force Base may notice increased aircraft activity as two MQ-9 Reaper aircraft are temporarily based there while construction continues at their home station, according to KVOA News 4.

KVOA News 4
