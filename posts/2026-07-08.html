🚨 Public Safety

Northwest Tucson deaths ruled apparent murder-suicide. The Pima County Sheriff's Department is investigating after a man and woman were found dead Tuesday in a home near Ina and Oldfather roads. According to KOLD, the man left a note on a neighbor's door after killing his wife before taking his own life.

Arizona Daily Star, KOLD News 13

Tucson woman gets justice after backlogged rape kit tested. A Tucson woman who was raped as a teenager received a conviction after DNA from her previously backlogged sexual assault kit was tested and matched a suspect, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Motorcyclist killed in southwest Tucson crash. A motorcyclist died and a passenger was injured in a crash early Sunday on Tucson's southwest side, according to the Arizona Daily Star. Authorities did not immediately release further details.

Arizona Daily Star

Pima County trails remain open despite extreme heat and recent hiker deaths. County officials told KOLD that closing trails would not be practical or enforceable, even as the region faces an Extreme Heat Warning and a string of recent hiker fatalities. Residents are urged to use caution.

KOLD News 13

Border Patrol seizes 71 pounds of cocaine and 19 pounds of fentanyl near Tubac. Agents made two separate drug seizures at the I-19 checkpoint north of Tubac, including 28 pounds of cocaine valued at approximately $456,000, after a canine alerted to a Hyundai Santa Fe, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

Former U of A basketball player released after fraud charge appearance. Former University of Arizona standout Kerr Kriisa was released following his first court appearance on federal wire fraud charges. Kriisa was arrested last week and is accused of defrauding at least two victims out of millions of dollars, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Measles outbreak confirmed at ICE detention facility in Florence. Seven active measles cases have been confirmed at ICE's Florence Detention Center in Pinal County, a Department of Homeland Security spokesperson said Tuesday. Florence is roughly 60 miles north of Tucson.

Arizona Daily Star, KOLD News 13

🏛️ Government

City of Tucson to open first shared south-side public safety complex. The city is set to open its first combined public safety facility on Tucson's south side, according to KVOA. The complex is designed to co-locate multiple public safety functions in an underserved area of the city.

KVOA News 4

Pima County Board of Supervisors moves to create tobacco retail licensing program. The board directed staff to develop a licensing program that would restrict tobacco and vape product sales to customers 21 and older, despite state law already setting that age limit, according to the Green Valley News.

Green Valley News

Mount Lemmon placed under Stage 3 water restrictions for second straight year. Mount Lemmon, in the Santa Catalina Mountains above Tucson, is again operating under its most stringent water use restrictions, KOLD reports, mirroring a designation imposed at the same point last year.

KOLD News 13

Oro Valley adopts 2.5% business use tax for second time. The Oro Valley Town Council has again adopted a first-ever 2.5% use tax on certain out-of-state purchases made by local businesses, according to Tucson Local Media. The measure had previously been adopted and apparently revisited by the council.

Tucson Local Media

DOJ threatens Arizona election officials with prosecution over noncitizen ballots. The U.S. Department of Justice sent a letter to the Arizona Secretary of State's Office warning of potential criminal prosecution for election officials who knowingly count ballots cast by noncitizens. Secretary of State Adrian Fontes called the letter "intimidation." Similar letters were sent to more than a dozen other states.

KOLD News 13, Arizona Mirror

🎓 Education

TUSD chronic absenteeism among worst in Arizona. Nearly half of students in Tucson Unified School District were chronically absent in the most recent state data, and the trend is worsening, according to KOLD. The district's absenteeism rate is worse than almost every other district in the state.

KOLD News 13

🏗️ Development & Business

Former Carson Middle School site cleared for 99-home development on Tucson's east side. The former Carson Middle School has been demolished to make way for a 99-lot Flexible Lot Development featuring single-family detached homes, according to KGUN 9. A permit for the project has been filed with the city.

KGUN 9

Dante's restaurant to close July 19, third Tucson closure since mid-June. Dante's on East Grant Road will hold a final whiskey dinner Sunday before permanently closing on July 19, the Arizona Daily Star reports. The closure marks the third Tucson restaurant to shut down since mid-June, reflecting a broader summer slowdown.

Arizona Daily Star

Construction near I-10 and Grant Road to continue into late September. A major construction project on Tucson's west side near Interstate 10 and Grant Road is expected to reach a major milestone in early August but will not be fully completed until late September, according to KVOA.

KVOA News 4

Trade agreement uncertainty raises concerns for Southern Arizona border economy. The United States' decision not to renew a border trade agreement covering the U.S., Mexico, and Canada introduces new uncertainty for the Southern Arizona economy, which depends heavily on cross-border commerce, according to KGUN 9.

KGUN 9
