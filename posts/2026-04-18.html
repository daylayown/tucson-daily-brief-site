April 18, 2026

🏛️ Government

Kelly and Gallego urge DHS to halt ICE detention facilities in Marana and Surprise. Arizona Sens. Mark Kelly and Ruben Gallego sent a letter Thursday to the Department of Homeland Security demanding a halt to two planned immigration detention centers — one in Surprise being scaled back from 1,500 to 542 beds, and one in Marana proposing to reopen a shuttered private prison as a 513-bed facility. The senators cited a lack of transparency and community impact assessments, and noted DHS never responded to a February letter on the same subject.

Arizona Mirror

Surprise ICE detention plan scaled back to 542 beds, opening pushed to October at earliest. A DHS plan to house up to 1,500 immigrants in a Surprise warehouse has been reduced to 542 detainees following local pushback, with DHS agreeing to pay the city $300,000 annually to offset lost property taxes. New DHS Secretary Markwayne Mullin has taken a more communicative approach than ousted predecessor Kristi Noem, though Democratic state lawmakers continue to urge Surprise to block the facility entirely.

Arizona Mirror

Trump rallies Arizona Republicans in Phoenix, endorses Biggs for governor. President Trump — whose approval ratings are underwater in Arizona — held a "Build The Red Wall" rally at Dream City Church in north Phoenix on Friday, hosted by Turning Point USA, where he endorsed Rep. Andy Biggs in the GOP gubernatorial primary against Rep. David Schweikert. Tucson-area Rep. Juan Ciscomani, a top Democratic target in 2026, addressed the crowd; Trump bragged about the ongoing U.S.-Israel war with Iran and alluded to possible action against Cuba.

Arizona Mirror

Kelly's legal team urges appeals court to preserve his Navy retirement rank. Arizona Sen. Mark Kelly's attorneys filed a brief this week urging the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals to uphold a lower court injunction blocking Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth's effort to strip Kelly's Navy captain retirement rank and pay. The case stems from a November video in which Kelly and other Democratic lawmakers with military backgrounds urged government employees to refuse illegal orders; oral arguments are scheduled for May 7.

🏗️ Development & Business

Bass Pro Shop opens first Tucson location. The outdoor and sporting goods retail chain has opened its first Tucson store — the third location in Arizona — bringing a major new retail anchor to the city. The store is the latest in a string of national retailers expanding their Tucson footprint.

Inside Tucson Business

Tucson small businesses adapt to tariff-driven economic uncertainty. Local independent businesses — including the 100-plus vendors at Midtown Mercantile Merchants — are recalibrating supply chains and pricing in response to new federal trade policies. The adjustments reflect a broader pattern of Tucson's small business community navigating a volatile economic environment.

🎉 Community & Events

Center for Cultural Organizing holds grand opening Sunday in Southside Tucson. The Southwest Folklife Alliance and Regeneración have transformed the historic Louis' Market at 4009 S. 12th Ave. into a resident-led space for community gathering and cultural exchange. A free public grand opening is scheduled for 6–9 p.m. Sunday, April 19.

Inside Tucson Business

Mosaic Quarter commissions six local muralists for Iceplex and Field House. The Tucson sports and entertainment complex has partnered with artists Ignacio Garcia, Jessica Gonzales, Pen Macias, Danny Martin, Rock Martinez, and Joe Pagac to create 11 original murals across its two upcoming venues. The Iceplex is slated to open next spring and the Field House next summer; the project is described as the largest simultaneous mural collaboration of its kind in the region.

Tucson Weekly

44th International Mariachi Conference showcases student musicians. Tucson's annual International Mariachi Conference is preparing for its 44th festival, which increasingly features women and young performers alongside the tradition's historical roots. The conference highlights Tucson's role as a center for mariachi education and cultural preservation.
