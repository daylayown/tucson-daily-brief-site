April 16, 2026

🚨 Public Safety

Four law enforcement shootings in eight days draw scrutiny. The Tucson area saw four officer-involved shootings between April 6 and April 13, two of them fatal — including the death of 15-year-old Joannie Jose Armenta, who was shot during a standoff after police say he raised what turned out to be a toy gun wrapped in cloth. Pima County Supervisor Matt Heinz, whose district covers all four incidents, called the cluster "a bit of an unusual burst of activity" but said the spike does not reflect broader crime trends; overall violent crime fell in Tucson and Pima County in 2025.

AZPM

Teen to be tried as adult in 3-year-old's street racing death. A 16-year-old driver will face adult charges in the death of 3-year-old Anna, killed when a car traveling at 87 mph struck her family's vehicle at Valencia Road and Country Club Drive. The teen and 22-year-old Christian Isiah Randall are both charged with second-degree murder; Anna's father is pushing for "Anna's Law," which would make street racing with injury or death a mandatory felony with required prison time.

KGUN 9

Pedestrian struck, killed crossing East Speedway. A man was fatally struck by a vehicle while crossing East Speedway near North Rosemont Boulevard on Tuesday night, Tucson police said. The crash adds to a growing number of pedestrian fatalities in the city this year.

Arizona Daily Star

🏛️ Government

Judge expected to rule today on Hernandez primary ballot eligibility. A Pima County Superior Court judge has until today to decide whether state Rep. Alma Hernandez can remain on July's Democratic primary ballot. Hernandez, who represents the 20th legislative district covering downtown Tucson, faces disqualification over $20,355 in unpaid campaign finance fines; her defense argues the fines are unenforceable under a 2018 Arizona Supreme Court ruling. Primary opponent and former Tucson city council member Rocque Perez says he will appeal to the state supreme court if the ruling goes against him.

AZPM

Court clears independent candidate for Arizona governor's race. A Maricopa County Superior Court judge dismissed the last remaining legal challenge to entrepreneur Hugh Lytle's 2026 gubernatorial candidacy on April 14, allowing him to remain on the ballot under the No Labels Party label. Two separate ballot challenges arguing his petitions failed to comply with state law were both rejected, with the court finding broad compliance with state statutes.

🎉 Community & Events

Rescued mountain lion cubs settle in at Desert Museum; public invited to name them. Two orphaned mountain lion cubs rescued from the Pacific Northwest have found a new home at the Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum. The public has a rare opportunity to help name the pair as they acclimate to their Tucson habitat.

Arizona Daily Star

Early heatwave fuels grasshopper explosion across Tucson. Strong winter rains followed by an unusually early heat spike have triggered a dramatic grasshopper population surge in the Tucson area. AZPM reports the combination created near-ideal breeding conditions, resulting in numbers well above typical spring levels.
