# Tucson Daily Brief — February 19, 2026

## 🏛️ Government

Tucson leaders push back on cuts to housing and drug programs amid $27 million deficit. During a Wednesday study session, Mayor and Council reaffirmed their commitment to nonprofit organizations and the city's public drug-use prevention initiatives as the city grapples with a $27 million budget shortfall this fiscal year. City CFO Anna Rosenberry presented four options totaling only $9.1 million in savings — a fraction of the deficit — including suspending the general fund portion of housing investment plans.

Arizona Luminaria / Tucson Agenda

South Tucson City Council votes to cancel license-plate reader contract. The council voted Wednesday to cancel its agreement with Flock Safety, a license-plate reader company, over concerns about the data the company shares with third parties.

Arizona Daily Star

Pima County supervisors adopt policies restricting ICE activity on county property. The Board of Supervisors adopted two new policies Tuesday limiting federal immigration enforcement on county-owned property and barring masked law enforcement officers from county facilities. Supporters called it a strong message to federal agents; GOP Supervisor Tim Christy dismissed the measures as "toothless."

AZPM / Tucson Agenda

Arizona AG appeals approval of Tucson Electric Power and Project Blue energy deal. Attorney General Kris Mayes filed a new legal appeal claiming the Arizona Corporation Commission surrendered its constitutional authority when it approved the energy agreement between TEP and the developers behind Project Blue. Separately, Mayes has also sought to reduce TEP's proposed 14 percent rate increase to 4 percent in proceedings before the commission.

Arizona Daily Star / Tucson Agenda

Marana rejects data center referendum petitions. The Town of Marana determined that citizen petitions seeking to put a proposed data center project to a public vote did not meet requirements under Arizona law, effectively clearing a path for the project to proceed without a referendum.

AZPM

Federal judge seizes control of Arizona prison healthcare. A federal judge Thursday ordered a takeover of the healthcare system inside Arizona's state prison, citing lost patience with the Department of Corrections, Rehabilitation and Reentry's repeated failures to meet court-mandated standards.

Arizona Daily Star

## 🚨 Public Safety

Nancy Guthrie case enters Day 19: Reward now $102,500, DNA yields no match, genealogy databases considered. An anonymous donor contributed $100,000 to 88-CRIME this week, boosting the total reward to $102,500 for information leading to an arrest. The Pima County Sheriff's Department confirmed that DNA recovered from gloves found two miles from Guthrie's home did not match any profile in national databases. Investigators say they may turn to genealogical DNA databases — a step that has cracked other high-profile cases — as the search for the missing Catalina Foothills woman continues. President Trump, in a brief interview aboard Air Force One Thursday, suggested it is "time to move on" from the sustained media coverage.

## 🎓 Education

U of A and nonprofit partner to build $250 million, 19-story dormitory. The University of Arizona announced a partnership with a nonprofit organization that will finance, build and operate a new 19-story student dormitory on the northwest corner of Speedway and Campbell avenues. The $250 million project will add significant on-campus housing capacity as the university works to expand its residential footprint.

Arizona Daily Star

## 🏗️ Development & Business

Mission Motor Collective closes after 11 months. The motorcycle-themed bar at 2 W. Grant Rd. announced an indefinite closure on social media Wednesday, citing high rent and the basic costs of operating the business. The venue opened in March 2025 and said it has "made some really amazing friends" but could not sustain operations. Owners did not rule out a future reopening.

Tucson Foodie

Arizona ACA marketplace enrollment drops by 66,000. A new report from health advocacy organization Protect Our Care found that 65,881 fewer Arizonans enrolled in coverage through the Affordable Care Act marketplace this year compared with 2025, a drop attributed to the expiration of enhanced federal premium tax credits. Health advocates warn the coverage gap will widen and could drive up costs for taxpayers as more uninsured residents seek care.

## 🎉 Community & Events

38th Tucson Bicycle Classic runs through Sunday. About 600 cyclists from across the U.S. and internationally kicked off the four-day Tucson Bicycle Classic on Thursday with a time trial in Marana on a portion of the Chuck Huckelberry Loop. Remaining stages include Friday's Sahuarita Road Race, Saturday's Rio Nuevo Tucson Criterium and Sunday's Oro Valley Circuit Race. Organizers say the event delivers a notable economic boost to the region.
