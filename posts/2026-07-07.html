⛈️ Weather — Tucson

⚠️ Extreme Heat Warning in effect from 10 AM today through 8 PM Wednesday. NWS Tucson warns of dangerously hot conditions with highs of 107–111°F across the Tucson metro area, Tohono O'odham Nation, and Western Pima County. Heat-related illness risk is significantly elevated.

Today: Sunny with a high near 110°F. A slight chance of afternoon showers/thunderstorms after 2 PM. North-northwest wind 1–6 mph. Heat index up to 108°F. Tonight: Partly cloudy, low near 80°F. Slight chance of storms before 8 PM. Southeast wind 2–7 mph. Wednesday: Sunny, high near 110°F again, with a slight chance of late-day storms after 5 PM. The Extreme Heat Warning continues through Wednesday evening. Outlook: Triple-digit highs persist through at least Friday, with highs near 107–110°F daily. Overnight lows remain near 80°F. Isolated late-afternoon and evening monsoon storms are possible each day later in the week, though chances remain slight (around 20%).

🚨 Public Safety

DNA grant yields 14 convictions in Tucson sexual assault cold cases. Testing of a backlog of rape kits, funded by a grant, identified more than 100 serial offenders and has resulted in 14 convictions in sexual assault cold cases in Tucson, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Goodwill shooting suspect's trial date set. Adrian Orozco, charged with murder in the shooting death of a young man during a robbery at a Tucson Goodwill donation center, appeared in Pima County Superior Court on Monday and had a trial date confirmed.

KGUN 9, KVOA

Motorcyclist killed in southwest Tucson crash. A motorcyclist was killed and a passenger was injured in a crash early Sunday on Tucson's southwest side, according to the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

One seriously injured in Pima County house fire. Northwest Fire Department crews responded to a fire involving two structures in Pima County, with one person seriously hurt, according to KOLD.

KOLD News 13

Dog killed in Tucson apartment fire. Tucson Fire Department crews responded to an early-morning fire at a Tucson apartment complex. The building was evacuated and firefighters prevented the blaze from spreading to neighboring units; one dog died in the fire.

KOLD News 13

Former U of A guard Kerr Kriisa arrested on federal fraud charges. Former University of Arizona point guard Kerr Kriisa was arrested by the FBI and faces wire fraud charges in connection with an alleged multimillion-dollar scheme, according to KGUN and KOLD.

KGUN 9, KOLD News 13

🏛️ Government

Pima County proposed budget adds $58 million in spending. Pima County's proposed 2026–27 budget would increase spending by $58 million, with the majority directed toward public safety and infrastructure, according to Tucson Spotlight. County supervisors are also debating employee pay raises.

Tucson Spotlight

Rio Nuevo draws on reserves after 25% revenue drop. Tucson's Rio Nuevo tax increment financing district is tapping its $21 million reserve fund for the first time in years following a projected 25% revenue decline, which district leaders partly attribute to Amazon's exemption from paying TIF taxes, according to Tucson Spotlight.

Tucson Spotlight

ESA school voucher oversight initiative headed to November ballot. A ballot initiative that would increase oversight of Arizona's Empowerment Scholarship Account (school voucher) program submitted more than 420,000 signatures to the secretary of state — well above the roughly 256,000 needed to qualify — and is likely headed to voters in November, according to the Nogales International.

Nogales International

Arizona disabled veterans to receive expanded property tax relief. New state legislation will provide Arizona disabled veterans with some of the broadest property tax relief benefits in the country, according to state House Majority Leader Michael Carbone, as reported by the Nogales International.

Nogales International

Medicaid restored for Planned Parenthood non-abortion services in Arizona. Arizonans enrolled in the state's Medicaid program are again eligible for non-abortion services through Planned Parenthood after a one-year federal ban included in the "One Big Beautiful Bill" expired July 4, according to KOLD and the Arizona Mirror.

KOLD News 13, Arizona Mirror

🏗️ Development & Business

Two longtime businesses close, citing Grant Road widening project. Two Tucson businesses have announced permanent closures, attributing the decisions to the impact of the ongoing Grant Road widening project. Dante's on East Grant Road will hold a final whiskey dinner Sunday before closing July 19; it is the third Tucson restaurant to close since mid-June, according to KVOA, KOLD, and the Arizona Daily Star.

KVOA, KOLD News 13, Arizona Daily Star

Cushing Street Skate Park breaks ground after decade of advocacy. Construction has begun on Tucson's Cushing Street Skate Park, which is set to become Arizona's first shaded skate park, situated beneath an Interstate 10 overpass. The project follows nearly ten years of community advocacy, according to Tucson Spotlight.

Tucson Spotlight

🎉 Community & Events

Saguaro City Music Theatre books 30 events at Berger Center. Saguaro City Music Theatre, which took over operation of the Berger Performing Arts Center at the Arizona Schools for the Deaf and the Blind last week, has already booked approximately 30 events at the Tucson theater, according to the Arizona Daily Star.

Arizona Daily Star

'A' Mountain fireworks show was the last of its kind. A behind-the-scenes look at this year's Fourth of July fireworks on Sentinel Peak — featuring nearly 1,000 aerial shells — reveals it was the final such show at that location, according to Arizona Luminaria.

Arizona Luminaria
