# Knight Foundation — Technology Product Strategist
## Cover Letter Draft (Nicholas De Leon)

**To:** recruiter@kf.org
**Subject:** Technology Product Strategist — Nicholas De Leon

---

Dear Ms. Nash,

For 20 years I've covered technology for digital newsrooms. For the last two, I've also been building it — shipping production AI tools at Consumer Reports and running an AI-assisted civic journalism platform that covers the Tucson metro. Your Technology Product Strategist posting is the first job description I've seen that values both halves of that work equally, and the combination is what I'd bring to the role.

**A builder track record at a national newsroom.** As Senior Reporter at Consumer Reports I've designed, built, and shipped a growing suite of editorial AI systems used by CR staff: a daily competitive intelligence brief for our Home Team editors, a weekly content freshness monitor running on a ~$4/month operating budget, and a custom Manifest V3 browser extension that solved our editorial price-tracking problem after conventional scraping kept failing. That experience has given me a working sense of what production AI in newsrooms actually requires — and, more relevantly to this role, what separates a fundable proposal from a press release.

**A civic journalism platform that maps directly to Knight's investment thesis.** Outside CR I run [Tucson Daily Brief](https://tucsondailybrief.com) — a daily news brief, AI-assisted agenda mining across four municipal governments (each on a different civic-tech vendor platform), a live AI meeting reporter built on Deepgram and Claude that captures local government livestreams in real time, and a human-reviewed news report pipeline. The whole operation runs in production at under $10/month. It's exactly the kind of model Press Forward was built to enable — except I built it myself, with explicit editorial guardrails that the recent Nota collapse made painfully relevant. I've done the practical work of figuring out what *responsible* AI-assisted local news actually looks like in production. Tucson isn't one of Knight's 26 communities, but the editorial model and operational architecture are directly transferable to any of them, and I'd like to bring that judgment to bear on the proposals crossing your desk.

**Editorial credibility and engineering literacy in one person.** The most common failure mode I see in newsroom AI hiring is parachuting a tech executive into an editorial context where they don't understand the reflexes that make a story a story — the Nota plagiarism scandal is only the most recent example. The reverse direction — a working journalist learning to ship — is much harder to fake but produces more durable results because the editorial trust comes pre-installed. I'd like to help make that the obvious path rather than the rare one, and Knight is the institution best positioned to shape which path the field actually takes.

I haven't worked on the funder side of the table before. I read your "junior or senior level" language as an invitation to candidates who would grow into the formal grant-management functions while bringing seniority on the substance, and that fits where I am. I also bring working proficiency in both Spanish (linguistic and subject-matter lead for the Spanish-language localization of Consumer Reports' Security Planner) and Portuguese — a combination I'm aware aligns well with Knight's Miami headquarters and the foundation's longstanding work across Spanish- and Portuguese-language media in Latin America. I'm based in southern Arizona and would happily relocate to Miami or work remotely from here, whichever serves the team better.

My resume is attached. [tucsondailybrief.com](https://tucsondailybrief.com) is the showcase project, and [daylayown.org](https://daylayown.org) has the rest. I'd welcome the chance to talk.

Thank you for reading,

**Nicholas De Leon**

## Notes for revision

- **Length:** ~520 words. Cover letters work best at ~400-500, but a substantive senior application can run a touch longer when it's earning the space. If you want to cut, the easiest paragraph to compress is the "Editorial credibility" one — it's the most "thesis-y" and could be 2 sentences instead of 4.
- **Customize before sending:** if you can find the name of the VP of Journalism in the next hour, replace "Dear Knight Foundation Journalism Team" with "Dear [Name]." If not, the team greeting is fine.
- **One missing thing worth adding** if you can: a single sentence referencing a recent Knight grant or initiative you specifically admire. It shows you've done homework. Examples to look for: a Knight News Challenge winner from 2025-2026, a Knight Lab project at Northwestern, a recent Knight + Lenfest co-funded grantee. Drop it into the "Civic journalism" paragraph as a one-liner like *"The kind of work Knight funded through [specific grant] is exactly the territory I've been mapping in practice."*
- **What NOT to include:** the Adobe joke. Even the constructive version of that argument should be left at "the most common failure mode" without naming companies or sounding bitter. The current draft does this — keep it that way.
- **Resume to attach:** the new tech-targeted version (`resume-tech.md` converted to PDF), NOT the journalism-targeted one with the Arizona Republic tagline.

## Subject line options
