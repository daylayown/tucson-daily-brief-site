Marana Town Council Meeting — May 19, 2026

Tucson Daily Brief Preview

This council meeting is relatively light on major development decisions but contains two significant budget-related actions and a notable closed-door legal session tied to ongoing litigation that Marana residents should watch. The agenda also touches on election administration and municipal operations heading into the next fiscal year.

Top Stories to Watch

1. 🏛️ Marana Closes Doors on Mandarina Lawsuit Settlement Talks

Item: E2 — Executive Session | Not on consent agenda

The council will meet privately to discuss settlement strategy in *Town of Marana v. Mandarina South, LLC*, a case pending in Pima County Superior Court. Mandarina is a massive mixed-use development project along the I-10 corridor in Marana's fastest-growing area, making any litigation touching it significant for residents and the regional development landscape. Settlement discussions suggest both sides may be looking for an off-ramp — the outcome could shape how thousands of planned homes and commercial acres get developed, and whether taxpayers foot any bill.

Reporter's note: Pull case number C20241394 from Pima County Superior Court records before the meeting. Determine what the dispute is specifically about — land use terms, infrastructure obligations, or something else — and whether a settlement could affect adjacent development approvals.

2. 💰 Council Sets Spending Blueprint for FY2027 with Tentative Budget Vote

Item: A2 — PUBLIC HEARING | Not on consent agenda

This is the only public hearing on the agenda, and it's a big one: the council will formally adopt Marana's tentative budget for fiscal year 2026-2027. As one of Arizona's fastest-growing municipalities, Marana's budget reflects critical decisions about staffing, infrastructure investment, and how the town is funding services for thousands of new residents. This is a rare opportunity for public comment on town spending priorities before the budget is finalized.

Reporter's note: Get the budget document ahead of the meeting. Key questions: How much is allocated for water infrastructure tied to new growth areas? Is there any line item connected to the proposed ICE detention facility discussions or data center development? Compare to last year's budget for growth in personnel and capital spending.

3. 📊 Town's Financial Health Gets a Mid-Year Check

Item: P1 — Presentation | Not on consent agenda

Finance Director Yiannis Kalaitzidis will present third-quarter results (July–March) for the General Fund and other major funds — essentially a public report card on whether Marana's revenues and spending are tracking as planned. In a high-growth town where sales tax receipts from new commercial development are a primary revenue driver, this snapshot matters for understanding whether the town's finances can keep pace with its rapid expansion.

Reporter's note: Watch for any surprises — shortfalls in development fee revenue, unexpected capital costs, or funds that are over- or under-budget. This presentation sets the context for the tentative budget vote immediately following.

4. 💸 Town Fees Are Going Up — Find Out Which Ones

Item: A1 — Ordinance No. 2026.009 | Not on consent agenda

The council will vote to amend Marana's Comprehensive Fee Schedule, which governs what residents and businesses pay for a wide range of town services, permits, and applications. Fee schedule changes can affect everything from building permits for new homes to park reservations to development application costs — directly impacting both everyday residents and the development industry driving Marana's growth.

Reporter's note: Obtain the fee schedule amendment document from the town clerk before the meeting. Identify which fees are increasing, by how much, and whether any new fees are being created. Development-related fee changes are particularly worth flagging given the pace of construction in the area.

5. 🗳️ Marana Locks In Election Partnership with Pima County for 2026

Item: C2 — Consent Agenda

The town will approve an intergovernmental agreement with Pima County for election services during the 2026 election cycle. While routine on its surface, this item is worth a second look given the ongoing controversy over the data center rezoning referendum — in which Marana residents' petition signatures were challenged and rejected, leading to lawsuits over ballot access rights.

Reporter's note: Confirm whether any terms of this IGA affect how future referendum petitions or town elections are administered. Given active litigation over voter petition rights in Marana, any changes to election administration carry added public interest.

6. 🏛️ Legislative Briefing Could Surface ICE Facility or Data Center Updates

Item: D1 — Discussion/Possible Action | Not on consent agenda

Town Manager Terry Rozema will brief the council on pending state, federal, and local government actions — a standing agenda item that frequently surfaces updates on the town's most contested issues. Given active federal activity around immigration enforcement and the unresolved ICE detention facility proposal at the former state prison site, as well as ongoing data center rezoning litigation, this discussion could yield significant news.

Reporter's note: This item often produces the most newsworthy off-the-cuff remarks of any meeting. Be in the room. Ask Rozema directly after the meeting about any federal communications regarding the detention facility.
