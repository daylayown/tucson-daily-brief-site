1. Federal "Big Beautiful Bill" Threatens to Kill Popular Mobile Food Market Serving Refugee Farmers, Food-Insecure Neighborhoods

Item 5 — PLAZA Mobile Market Presentation | Study Session

The UA Garden Kitchen's PLAZA Mobile Market, which has operated weekly at the Valencia and Quincie Douglas library branches, is at risk of shutting down entirely after cuts to the SNAP-Ed program embedded in the federal reconciliation bill (HR1). The program is a rare two-fer: it expands fresh food access in underserved communities *and* provides economic lifelines to small refugee farmers in the Tucson area. This is a concrete, local example of how the federal bill is landing in Pima County — the kind of story that puts a human face on abstract budget fights in Washington. Watch whether supervisors signal any interest in county bridge funding to keep the program alive.

Note: Study session item — no vote today, but direction given here could lead to future action.
