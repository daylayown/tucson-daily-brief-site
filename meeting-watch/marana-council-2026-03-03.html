Town Finance Director Yiannis Kalaitzidis will present proposed changes to Marana's Comprehensive Fee Schedule, covering a wide range of rates and fees charged to residents and businesses. Fee schedule revisions in fast-growing towns like Marana typically affect development permit costs, parks and recreation fees, utility rates, and other everyday municipal charges. This is a presentation and discussion item — no vote tonight — but it's the public's best early opportunity to weigh in before changes are adopted, making it worth watching closely for what's actually on the table.

2. New Tax District Could Fund Infrastructure for Major Development

Action Item D2 | Not on Consent Agenda

Headline: Council to Vote on Creating Special Tax District for "Cascada" Development

The council will consider formally establishing the Cascada Community Facilities District (CFD), a financing mechanism that allows a specific geographic area — almost certainly tied to a large master-planned residential or mixed-use development — to levy its own taxes or assessments to fund roads, utilities, and other infrastructure. CFDs are a common tool in Arizona's booming exurbs, but they shift infrastructure costs onto future homebuyers within the district rather than the broader town. Residents considering buying in any Cascada-area development would effectively be taxed through this district on top of normal property taxes, making the terms and boundaries of this vote significant.

Action Item D1 | Not on Consent Agenda

Headline: Staff to Brief Council on Full Slate of Active Development Projects

Planning Director Jason Angell will update the council on all current and proposed projects tracked on the town's development dashboard — giving the clearest public snapshot of what's in the pipeline along Marana's rapidly growing I-10 corridor and beyond. These updates frequently surface new residential subdivisions, commercial projects, or infrastructure proposals before they formally hit the agenda, making this a key item for residents and regional observers tracking Marana's expansion. Watch for any projects near the Tangerine Road corridor, Hartman Vistoso area, or other active growth zones.

4. Police Records System Gets State Funding — But Details Matter

Consent Item C1 | On Consent Agenda ⚠️

Headline: Marana Police to Tap State Grant for Records and Data-Sharing System

The council is set to approve an agreement with the Arizona Department of Administration to fund a law enforcement records management and data-sharing system for the Marana Police Department. While framed as routine, data-sharing agreements between police agencies carry real civil liberties implications — particularly in the current environment where Marana is also the site of a proposed ICE immigration detention facility. The consent agenda placement means it will pass without discussion unless a council member pulls it.
