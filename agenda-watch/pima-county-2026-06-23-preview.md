### 3. County May Crack Down on Tobacco Sales to Minors — New Licensing Program Proposed
**Item 31 | Agenda Item — District 2**

A supervisor is asking the Board to direct county staff to develop a Tobacco Retail Licensing program that would regulate the sale of tobacco products to people under 21. The county's Health Department drafted a similar program in 2019, but the Board at that time declined to move forward; this item would revive that effort and require staff to return with a formal implementation proposal by late fall 2026. If adopted, businesses selling tobacco in unincorporated Pima County could face new licensing fees, inspections, and penalties — a significant new regulatory layer for retailers.

> 📌 Worth noting: The City of Tucson already has a similar ordinance; this would extend comparable protections to unincorporated areas.

### 4. $2.2 Million for Opioid Sobering Center — But Is It Enough?
**Item 44 | Contract — Community Bridges, Inc.**

The Board is set to approve a $2.2 million contract amendment keeping the Pima County SAFR Center sobering facility running through June 2027, funded by Arizona's share of national opioid litigation settlements. The SAFR Center provides an alternative to jail or emergency rooms for people experiencing acute intoxication or overdose, making it a key piece of the county's behavioral health infrastructure. Given Tucson's ongoing opioid crisis, the size of this contract and its funding source (one-time settlement dollars, not a recurring revenue stream) raises questions about long-term sustainability.

> 📋 **On regular agenda** — will require a vote but could pass without extended debate.
