### 2. New Tax District Could Fund Infrastructure for Major Development
**Action Item D2 | Not on Consent Agenda**

**Headline: Council to Vote on Creating Special Tax District for "Cascada" Development**

The council will consider formally establishing the **Cascada Community Facilities District (CFD)**, a financing mechanism that allows a specific geographic area — almost certainly tied to a large master-planned residential or mixed-use development — to levy its own taxes or assessments to fund roads, utilities, and other infrastructure. CFDs are a common tool in Arizona's booming exurbs, but they shift infrastructure costs onto future homebuyers within the district rather than the broader town. Residents considering buying in any Cascada-area development would effectively be taxed through this district on top of normal property taxes, making the terms and boundaries of this vote significant.
